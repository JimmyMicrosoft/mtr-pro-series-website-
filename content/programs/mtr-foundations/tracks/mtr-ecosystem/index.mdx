## Overview

Every MTR project starts with the same question: do you actually understand the platform you are deploying? Not just how to cable it or click through the setup wizard — but what it is, how it works at a system level, why it exists in the form it does, and what your role is versus the IT team's role. This track answers those questions.

The MTR Ecosystem track is the foundational entry point for the entire MTR Pro Series curriculum. It covers the Microsoft Teams Rooms platform from first principles — its history, its two distinct runtime environments, the hardware ecosystem you will be selecting components from, the licensing model that governs feature availability, and the meeting infrastructure it sits on top of. None of this is abstract theory. Every topic in this track maps directly to something you will encounter on real MTR projects: a client asking why they should choose MTR over Zoom Rooms, a resource account that won't accept meeting invites, a room that won't appear on the touch console calendar, a Teams Admin Center health check during commissioning.

The track is organized into two modules. The first, *Introduction to Microsoft Teams Rooms*, covers the platform itself — what it is, how it is licensed, and what you as an AV integrator are responsible for. The second, *Microsoft Teams Fundamentals for AV Professionals*, covers how Teams meetings work and what that means for room design and configuration. These two bodies of knowledge together form the complete foundation every subsequent track in the MTR Pro Series builds on.

This track is designed for AV integrators at the start of their MTR journey — field technicians scoping their first MTR project, systems programmers transitioning from other platforms, IT support staff who need a practitioner-level understanding of room systems, and pre-sales consultants who need to speak about MTR with authority. No prior experience with Microsoft Teams Rooms is required.

## What you will learn

By completing this track, you will be able to:

- Explain the origin, architecture, and market position of Microsoft Teams Rooms, and compare it credibly to Zoom Rooms and Cisco Webex Rooms in client conversations
- Differentiate MTR Windows from MTR Android across architecture, feature availability, and update mechanisms, and select the correct platform for a given deployment scenario
- Navigate the Microsoft Teams Rooms certified hardware catalog, apply room size guidance, and compose a preliminary bill of materials for small, medium, and large rooms
- Describe the Teams Rooms Basic and Pro license tiers, explain the resource account model, and identify the licensing stack required for PSTN calling from a room
- Define the boundary between AV integrator and IT administrator responsibilities, and write clear scope-of-work language for an MTR project
- Identify the four Microsoft Teams meeting types and explain how each affects the in-room AV experience and room design requirements
- Describe the four primary MTR meeting join methods — calendar one-touch, proximity join, manual meeting ID, and BYOD — and their configuration requirements
- Navigate the Teams Admin Center to verify device health, peripheral status, meeting policy assignment, and device app version after installation
- Diagnose and remediate the three most common resource account misconfigurations using PowerShell verification commands

## Prerequisites

This track has no prerequisites. It is the first track in the MTR Foundations program and the recommended entry point for all learners beginning their Microsoft Teams Rooms training.

You do not need prior experience with Microsoft Teams Rooms to begin this track. General AV integration experience is helpful context but is not required.

If you are an experienced AV integrator who has previously deployed Skype for Business Room System or early-generation MTR systems, this track will formalize your knowledge and update it for current platform releases, licensing models, and admin tooling. Much of the hardware and configuration content will be new; some of the platform overview content may be review.

## Modules in this track

This track contains **2 modules** across **9 lessons**, with an estimated total duration of **4 hours 30 minutes**.

### Module 1: Introduction to Microsoft Teams Rooms

**Duration:** 135 min (2 hrs 15 min) | **Lessons:** 5 | **Difficulty:** Beginner

This module establishes your working model of the Microsoft Teams Rooms platform. You will learn what MTR is and where it came from, how the Windows and Android variants differ in architecture and feature support, how to navigate the certified hardware ecosystem and select components for different room sizes, how the licensing model works and what the resource account requires, and where your scope of work as an AV integrator begins and ends.

Every lesson in this module prepares you for work you will do on every MTR project. The licensing lesson alone will prevent the single most common pre-deployment failure — a resource account that is not correctly configured before device sign-in.

[Begin Module 1: Introduction to Microsoft Teams Rooms →](/programs/mtr-foundations/tracks/mtr-ecosystem/modules/introduction-to-teams-rooms/)

---

### Module 2: Microsoft Teams Fundamentals for AV Professionals

**Duration:** 135 min (2 hrs 15 min) | **Lessons:** 4 | **Difficulty:** Beginner

This module covers the meeting infrastructure that MTR sits on top of. You will learn the four Microsoft Teams meeting types and what each means for AV design, the four ways a room can join a meeting and what each requires, how to use the Teams Admin Center to verify device health and policy configuration after installation, and how resource accounts work at a technical level — including the PowerShell commands integrators use to verify them.

This module bridges the gap between "installed the hardware" and "the room works." The gap between those two states is almost always a configuration issue in Teams or the resource account — and this module explains exactly what to look for.

[Begin Module 2: Microsoft Teams Fundamentals for AV Professionals →](/programs/mtr-foundations/tracks/mtr-ecosystem/modules/teams-fundamentals-for-av/)

## Track assessment

The **MTR Ecosystem Track Assessment** is a 25-question test covering both modules in this track. You must score 80% or higher to earn the MTR Ecosystem credential. You may attempt the assessment up to 3 times.

The assessment covers:
- Platform history and competitive positioning (Lesson 1.1.1)
- MTR Windows vs. Android feature parity (Lesson 1.1.2)
- Certified hardware selection and room sizing (Lesson 1.1.3)
- Licensing tiers and resource account configuration (Lessons 1.1.4 and 1.2.4)
- Integrator vs. IT administrator scope (Lesson 1.1.5)
- Teams meeting types and AV implications (Lesson 1.2.1)
- Meeting join methods and configuration requirements (Lesson 1.2.2)
- Teams Admin Center device management (Lesson 1.2.3)
