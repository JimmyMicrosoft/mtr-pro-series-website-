## Overview

Microsoft Teams Rooms is a purpose-built conferencing application that transforms certified hardware into a first-class Microsoft Teams endpoint. Before you can install, configure, or troubleshoot an MTR system competently, you need a solid working model of what the platform is, how it is structured, and why clients choose it. That is what this module builds.

This module covers the platform from the ground up — its history and market context, the two distinct runtime environments (Windows and Android), the certified hardware ecosystem you will be selecting components from on every project, the licensing model that governs what features are available in each room, and finally, your role as an AV integrator versus the IT administrator's role. These five topics collectively form the foundation for everything else in the MTR Pro Series curriculum.

By the time you complete this module, you will be able to walk into a client meeting, speak confidently about MTR's position relative to competitors, ask the right questions about a client's Microsoft 365 environment and room requirements, and scope an AV integrator's responsibilities clearly in writing. These are not theoretical skills — they are the practical competencies that distinguish an integrator who knows MTR from one who is guessing.

This module is the first of two in the MTR Ecosystem track. It focuses on the platform itself. The second module, [Microsoft Teams Fundamentals for AV Professionals](/programs/mtr-foundations/tracks/mtr-ecosystem/modules/teams-fundamentals-for-av/), focuses on how Teams meetings work and what that means for room design and configuration.

---

## What you will learn

By completing this module, you will be able to:

- Describe the Microsoft Teams Rooms platform, its history, and its market position relative to Zoom Rooms and Cisco Webex Rooms
- Select the correct MTR platform (Windows or Android) for a given room scenario based on room size, feature requirements, and hardware constraints
- Navigate the Microsoft certified hardware catalog and build a preliminary bill of materials for a small, medium, or large room
- Explain the Teams Rooms Basic and Pro license tiers and describe the correct resource account configuration required before device sign-in
- Define the AV integrator's scope of work and construct a responsibility matrix distinguishing integrator tasks from IT administrator tasks

This module has no prerequisites. It is the first module in the MTR Ecosystem track and the recommended starting point for all learners beginning the MTR Foundations program.
