## Overview

An MTR room is not an island. Every room in your deployment is connected to a Microsoft Teams environment — a tenant with specific meeting policies, user accounts, calendar configurations, and an admin console that governs how devices behave. To install and commission MTR rooms professionally, you need to understand that environment: how Teams meetings are structured, how MTR devices join them, what the Teams Admin Center looks like from an integrator's perspective, and how resource accounts must be configured before a device will function.

This is the module that bridges the gap between hardware knowledge and platform knowledge. Module 1.1 gave you the device and licensing foundation. This module gives you the Teams-side context that makes that hardware actually work in a client environment. The questions answered here come up on every MTR project — why does the room show no meetings, what does that red indicator mean in the admin center, why won't the room join this particular meeting type — and the integrators who can answer them confidently are the ones clients call back.

---

## What you will learn

By completing this module, you will be able to:

- Distinguish the four primary Teams meeting types (channel, private, webinar, town hall) and describe how each affects MTR join behavior and AV requirements
- Explain the four join methods available on MTR (calendar one-touch, proximity join, manual ID entry, BYOD) and identify the prerequisites for each
- Navigate the Teams Admin Center to verify device health, peripheral status, meeting policy assignment, and app version after installation
- Verify resource account configuration using the Teams Admin Center and PowerShell, and identify the most common misconfigurations that prevent devices from working
