## Overview

Track 1 gave you the platform knowledge — what MTR is, how it works, and how it connects to Microsoft 365. Track 2 is where the work becomes physical. This track covers everything required to turn a blank conference room into a commissioned MTR space: surveying the site, planning the infrastructure, running cable, terminating and testing, mounting hardware, and building the rack.

Physical installation quality determines whether an MTR room stays reliable for five years or generates support calls starting in week two. The skills in this track are not abstract — they are hands-on competencies that show up in every room you build. Integrators who take this seriously produce rooms that commissioners, clients, and IT administrators are confident in. Integrators who skip the fundamentals produce rooms that work until they don't.

---

## What you will learn

By completing this track, you will be able to:

- Conduct a structured pre-installation site survey and produce a documentation package that supports accurate system design and cost estimation
- Assess a site's electrical, network, and AV pathway infrastructure and identify deficiencies before installation begins
- Produce a complete, accurate Bill of Materials with certified hardware and properly quantified cable and consumables
- Identify and apply the correct cable type for each MTR signal path, including network, video, USB, and audio
- Terminate, test, and document Cat6/6A cable to TIA-568 standards using professional tooling
- Mount displays, cameras, microphones, touch consoles, and compute units correctly for the room geometry and use case
- Design and populate an AV equipment rack following professional standards for thermal management, cable dressing, and serviceability
