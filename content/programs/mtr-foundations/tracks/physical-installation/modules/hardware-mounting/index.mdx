## Overview

Planning and cabling are now behind you. Module 2.3 is where the hardware goes on the wall, in the ceiling, on the table, and in the rack. This is the work that clients see — the display centered and level at exactly the right height, the camera framing every seat, the touch console perfectly positioned at the table, the rack dressed and organized. Done correctly, hardware mounting produces a room that functions reliably and looks professional. Done poorly, it generates complaints from day one and expensive remediation work.

This module covers all four hardware placement disciplines: display mounting, compute unit and touch console placement, camera and microphone positioning, and rack design.

---

## What you will learn

By completing this module, you will be able to:

- Select the correct VESA mount type, verify structural attachment requirements, and position a display at the correct height and tilt for the room geometry
- Position an MTR touch console for ADA compliance and cable concealment, and place the compute unit with adequate ventilation and maintenance access
- Position a camera to achieve correct horizontal centering, vertical height, and field-of-view coverage for the seating area
- Place microphone elements (tabletop, ceiling array) to achieve full coverage with appropriate overlap while avoiding HVAC interference
- Produce a rack elevation drawing and install equipment following thermal management, cable dressing, and AVIXA E502.01 rack design standards
