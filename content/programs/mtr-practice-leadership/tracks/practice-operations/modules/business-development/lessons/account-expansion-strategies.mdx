## Account Expansion as the Most Cost-Effective Growth Source

Acquiring a new client costs 5–7× more than growing revenue from an existing one. The most commercially efficient growth strategy for an MTR practice is to maximise revenue from existing clients before investing heavily in new logo acquisition.

Account expansion is not about pressuring clients to spend more — it is about identifying genuine additional needs and meeting them. Clients who are expanded sustainably become long-term advocates; clients who feel over-sold churn.

---

## The Land-and-Expand Model

The land-and-expand model structures the initial engagement as a beachhead that creates the conditions for larger subsequent engagement.

**Stage 1: Land**
Win a specific, scoped initial engagement — typically a pilot or first-site deployment. Objective: demonstrate capability, establish trust, identify the full expansion opportunity.

**Stage 2: Expand**
Convert the initial engagement to a larger programme — more rooms, more sites, managed service, or adjacent services. Objective: deepen the relationship and grow recurring revenue.

**Stage 3: Maximise**
Become the client's primary technology partner for the relevant domain. Objective: protect the account from competitive displacement and continue growing within the account.

## Expansion Opportunity Types

### Upsell: More of the Same

**What**: More rooms, more sites, more licenses, higher service tier.

**Triggers**:
- Room utilisation data shows high demand rooms (>75% utilisation)
- New office space, floor, or building in the client's estate
- Acquisition or merger bringing new sites
- Client expands headcount and needs more meeting capacity

**Conversation opener**: "Your utilisation data shows three rooms consistently at 80% booking capacity. That means your teams can't always get a room when they need one. Based on the floor plans you shared, there are two additional spaces on Floor 3 that could be equipped with the same configuration. Would it be useful to model what that would cost?"

### Cross-sell: Adjacent Services

**What**: Services adjacent to MTR that the practice can deliver.

**Common cross-sell opportunities for MTR practices**:

| Adjacent Service | Connection to MTR | Client Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| Microsoft 365 managed service (broader) | MTR is a subset of M365 | Single vendor for all M365 support |
| Teams Phone / PSTN calling | MTR devices can use Teams Phone | Unified calling and meeting system |
| Digital signage | Same displays, different content | Lobby/wayfinding digital signage |
| Visitor management | Shared physical infrastructure | Front desk check-in systems |
| Acoustic treatment | Prerequisite for good MTR audio | Better meeting quality |
| Structured cabling | Infrastructure for MTR | Whole-building network refresh |

**Conversation opener**: "Now that the MTR rooms are delivering well, are there other collaboration technology needs we could help with? I noticed your reception area still has a static notice board — we can run Teams digital signage on the same infrastructure."

### Tier Upgrade: Managed Service Upsell

**What**: Moving a client from Bronze to Silver or Silver to Gold managed service.

**Triggers**:
- Client reports a P1 incident outside their current service hours
- Client's IT team struggles with recurring issues that require more proactive management
- Client has a high-stakes event (board meeting, AGM) that requires guaranteed coverage
- Client champion becomes a senior stakeholder with higher expectations

**Conversation opener**: "You had a room go offline on a Friday evening last month and it wasn't picked up until Monday. Under your current Silver plan, that's expected — support is Mon–Fri extended hours. If that kind of availability gap is a concern, Gold covers weekend monitoring and same-day response. Given the executive meetings in those rooms, it might be worth the additional investment."

## Account Expansion Process

### Identify

Use the QBR utilisation data and customer health score to identify which accounts have expansion potential. An account that is:
- Healthy (Green health score)
- High utilisation (>50% average)
- Actively using the managed service

...is ready for an expansion conversation.

### Prioritise

Not every client should be the focus of expansion activity simultaneously. Prioritise accounts with:
- Most rooms under management (larger base = more expansion value)
- Highest growth potential (new offices, acquisitions, growing headcount)
- Strongest sponsor relationship (advocate who will champion internal approval)

### Approach

Expansion conversations should feel like advisory conversations, not sales pitches:

1. **Present the data**: "Here's what the utilisation shows across your estate..."
2. **Identify the need**: "Based on this, the gap appears to be [X]..."
3. **Propose a solution**: "We'd recommend [approach] for the following reasons..."
4. **Make the ask**: "Would it make sense to put together a scope for this?"

Never make an expansion proposal in the middle of an ongoing service issue. Resolve the issue first; then, when the client is in a positive mindset, have the growth conversation.

## Account Expansion Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | Total revenue from existing clients this period ÷ same clients last period × 100 | >110% (growth from existing base) |
| Account expansion rate | % of managed service clients where revenue grew YoY | >40% of accounts |
| Upsell revenue | Revenue from expansion in existing accounts | >30% of total revenue |
| Time to first expansion | Days from initial deployment to first expansion engagement | &lt;12 months |

An NRR >100% means the managed service base is growing even without new client acquisition — existing clients are generating more revenue than they did last year. This is the most powerful indicator of a healthy managed service business.
