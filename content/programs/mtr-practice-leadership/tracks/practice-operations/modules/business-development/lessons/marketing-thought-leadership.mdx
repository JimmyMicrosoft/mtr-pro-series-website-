## Marketing as Pipeline Investment

Marketing exists to generate pipeline — specifically, qualified inbound opportunities where the client has already identified a need and believes the practice can solve it. Inbound leads close at higher rates and with less sales cost than cold outbound.

For a specialist practice like an MTR provider, marketing is also about establishing authority. In a market where many IT resellers claim to deliver MTR, a practice that demonstrably knows more than competitors builds the trust that converts enquiries into clients.

---

## Marketing Activity Categories

### Content Marketing and Thought Leadership

**What it is**: Publishing valuable technical content that demonstrates expertise and helps potential clients understand their challenges.

**Why it works**: Clients researching MTR deployments search for answers before they speak to vendors. Content that answers their questions positions the practice as the authoritative source — and as the natural first call.

**Content types for an MTR practice**:

| Content Type | Topic Examples | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Technical blog posts | "How to deploy MTR at enterprise scale with Intune"; "MTR vs Zoom Rooms in 2026" | Practice website, LinkedIn |
| Case studies | "How [client type] deployed 50 MTR rooms and improved hybrid meeting equity" | Website, email, proposals |
| Videos | Room demonstrations; "How to use your new MTR room" (shareable) | YouTube, LinkedIn, client intranets |
| Webinars | "Planning your first MTR deployment"; "Managing MTR at scale" | LinkedIn, partner programme |
| White papers/guides | "The CIO's guide to MTR ROI"; "MTR network readiness checklist" | Gated download (lead generation) |

**Minimum viable content programme**: Two blog posts per month + one case study per quarter.

<Callout type="tip">
**The best content comes directly from the practice's technical work.** Every time an engineer solves a novel problem, discovers a new integration, or documents a design pattern — that is a content asset. Build the habit of converting operational knowledge to published content.
</Callout>

### LinkedIn Presence

LinkedIn is the primary professional marketing channel for B2B technology practices. Effective LinkedIn strategy for an MTR practice:

- **Personal thought leadership**: The practice leader posting 2–3 times per week on MTR topics, hybrid work, and Microsoft ecosystem developments. Personal posts significantly outperform company page posts.
- **Employee advocacy**: Encourage engineers to share technical content from their own profiles. An engineer posting about a problem they solved is authentic and credible.
- **Company page**: Share case studies, job postings, partner announcements, and product news.
- **LinkedIn Articles**: Long-form technical content indexed by LinkedIn's search algorithm.

### Reference Programme

Satisfied clients are the most powerful marketing asset. A formal reference programme:

1. Identify clients with strong results and positive relationships (health score Green + high utilisation)
2. Request permission to create a named case study
3. Offer the client value in return: a feature in the case study (visibility), or reference call support for their own credibility with peers
4. Use reference clients in proposals, on the website, and in partner programme submissions

**Ideal case study structure**:
- Client context (industry, size, challenge)
- What was deployed (rooms, design patterns, management approach)
- Outcomes achieved (utilisation %, productivity improvement, IT overhead reduction)
- Client quote from a named senior stakeholder

### Events and Speaking

Speaking at industry events positions the practice as a thought leader, not just a vendor:

- **Microsoft events**: Inspire, Ignite, Microsoft Tech Community events — partner tracks actively seek speakers with real-world deployment expertise
- **AV industry events**: ISE, InfoComm — opportunities to reach facilities managers and AV integrators
- **Client events**: Host a "Hybrid Work Showcase" at the practice's premises or at a Microsoft Technology Centre — invite IT leaders from prospect clients

## Microsoft Partner Marketing Resources

As a Microsoft Solutions Partner, the practice has access to marketing resources:

- **Microsoft Partner directory listing**: Visible to organisations searching for Microsoft certified partners via the partner locator
- **Co-branded marketing assets**: Microsoft-approved logos, case study templates, and product imagery
- **MDF (Market Development Funds)**: Budget for joint marketing activities — apply through the partner centre
- **Microsoft Technology Centres**: High-spec demo environments available for hosting client showcase events
- **Microsoft co-marketing campaigns**: Join coordinated campaigns aligned with product launches (e.g., new Teams Rooms features)

Utilising these resources amplifies practice marketing at minimal cost.

## Measuring Marketing Effectiveness

Marketing investment should be tracked against pipeline contribution:

| Metric | Measurement | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Inbound enquiries | Monthly lead count | Growing YoY |
| Inbound lead to qualified | % of inbound that reach Stage 2+ | >30% |
| Content-sourced revenue | Revenue attributed to content-originated enquiries | >20% of new logo revenue |
| Website traffic | Monthly unique visitors | Growing QoQ |
| LinkedIn engagement | Post impressions, profile views, connection requests | Trending up |
| Cost per qualified lead | Total marketing spend ÷ qualified leads | Declining as efficiency improves |

Track which content pieces generate enquiries (use UTM parameters in links) and invest more in the formats that convert.

## Building a Marketing Habit

The most common reason practices don't market consistently is that it falls to people who have day jobs. Marketing only happens when there's time, which means it never happens consistently.

**Solutions**:
- Assign marketing as a named responsibility with a time budget (e.g., 20% of the practice manager's time)
- Block content creation time in the calendar before it fills with reactive work
- Use a content calendar — 3-month content plan that prevents blank-page paralysis
- Repurpose content across channels: one technical blog post becomes three LinkedIn posts, a video script, and a proposal leave-behind
