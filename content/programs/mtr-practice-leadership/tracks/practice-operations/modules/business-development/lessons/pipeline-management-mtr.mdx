## Pipeline Visibility as a Practice Health Indicator

A practice that cannot see its pipeline clearly is operating blind. Pipeline management provides:

- **Revenue forecasting**: What will we invoice in the next 30/60/90 days?
- **Resource planning**: When will we need installation and commissioning capacity?
- **Growth management**: Are we winning enough new business to replace completed projects?
- **Risk identification**: Is there a gap in the pipeline that will affect revenue 60 days from now?

The pipeline is not just a sales tool — it is a practice management tool.

## Pipeline Stage Definitions

Define stages that reflect the actual MTR sales process. Probability estimates at each stage inform weighted pipeline calculations.

| Stage | Definition | Probability | Typical Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0. Suspect | Potential opportunity identified; no contact made | 5% | Indefinite |
| 1. Prospect | Initial conversation; client has a stated need | 15% | 2–4 weeks |
| 2. Qualified | MEDDIC qualification completed; economic buyer identified; budget confirmed | 30% | 2–4 weeks |
| 3. Site Survey | Site survey completed; NRA in progress; active engagement | 50% | 2–4 weeks |
| 4. Proposal | Proposal submitted and presented | 65% | 2–4 weeks |
| 5. Negotiation | Proposal accepted in principle; commercial terms being finalised | 85% | 1–3 weeks |
| 6. Closed Won | Contract signed | 100% | — |
| 7. Closed Lost | Opportunity lost; record reason | 0% | — |

### Managed Service Renewal Pipeline

Managed service renewals are a separate pipeline category:

| Stage | Definition | Probability |
|---|---|---|
| R1. Auto-renewal expected | Contract due; client health score Green; no action needed | 95% |
| R2. Active renewal | Contract due; client health score Amber; renewal conversation in progress | 75% |
| R3. At-risk renewal | Contract due; client health score Red; recovery plan in progress | 30% |
| R4. Not renewing | Client confirmed non-renewal | 0% |

## Pipeline Metrics

### Weighted Pipeline Value

`Weighted pipeline = Σ (Deal value × Stage probability)`

This gives a realistic revenue forecast that accounts for deal stage, not just deal size.

**Example**:
```
Deal A: £50,000 at Stage 4 (65%) = £32,500
Deal B: £30,000 at Stage 5 (85%) = £25,500
Deal C: £80,000 at Stage 3 (50%) = £40,000
Deal D: £20,000 at Stage 2 (30%) = £6,000

Total weighted pipeline: £104,000
```

Compare this against the practice's target monthly revenue to assess whether the pipeline is healthy.

### Pipeline Coverage Ratio

`Coverage ratio = Weighted pipeline value / Target revenue for period`

Target: 3× coverage for a 30-day period. If you want to close £50,000 in 30 days, you should have £150,000 in the weighted pipeline for that period.

Coverage <2× is a warning sign — the pipeline needs building immediately.

### Win Rate

`Win rate = Closed Won ÷ (Closed Won + Closed Lost) × 100`

Track win rates by:
- Deal size (are small or large deals harder to win?)
- Source (referral vs outbound vs inbound)
- Competitor (what is the win rate when Zoom Rooms is the alternative?)

Declining win rates indicate a change in competitive position or proposal quality — investigate root cause.

## Pipeline Cadence

### Weekly Pipeline Review

The practice manager or commercial lead reviews the pipeline weekly:

1. Review all deals in Stages 3–5: What has moved forward? What is stalled? Why?
2. Identify any deals that have not progressed in 2+ weeks: are they truly active or should they be moved to suspect?
3. Review coverage ratio: are we on track for next 30/60 days?
4. Identify any new opportunities that need to be qualified

### Monthly Pipeline Health Check

1. Win rate for the month: is it trending in the right direction?
2. Average deal size: is it growing (upsell success) or declining (commoditisation)?
3. Time in stage: are deals stalling at a particular stage? That stage is where the sales process has a problem.
4. New opportunities added: is the top of the funnel being fed?

## Pipeline Health Problems and Solutions

### Too Thin at the Top (Few Suspects and Prospects)

**Symptom**: Coverage ratio drops because no new opportunities are entering the pipeline.

**Causes**: No outbound prospecting activity; over-reliance on inbound; account managers focused on existing clients only.

**Solutions**: Dedicated outbound prospecting time each week; referral campaign to existing clients; partner co-sell with Microsoft PAM.

### Stalled Deals in Proposal Stage

**Symptom**: Multiple deals stuck at Stage 4 for >4 weeks.

**Causes**: Proposal not compelling; economic buyer not engaged; internal client politics; budget approval process delayed.

**Solutions**: Request a presentation meeting (don't let proposals die by email); confirm economic buyer has seen the proposal; use the timeline close (project timeline creates urgency).

### Low Win Rate

**Symptom**: Fewer than 35% of qualified opportunities are converting to Closed Won.

**Causes**: Competing on price against better-positioned competitors; proposals not differentiated; going into deals too late (incumbent has advantage).

**Solutions**: Review win/loss data; improve qualification (don't pursue deals you can't win); strengthen presales technical credibility.
