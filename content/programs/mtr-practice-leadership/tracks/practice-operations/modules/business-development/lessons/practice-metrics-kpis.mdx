## Metrics as a Management Instrument

A practice without metrics is run on instinct and memory. Instinct and memory become unreliable as the practice grows — more clients, more engineers, more complexity. Metrics convert intuition into evidence.

The practice dashboard provides a monthly snapshot of practice health across four dimensions:

1. **Financial**: Is the business generating sustainable profit?
2. **Pipeline**: Is there enough future business to sustain growth?
3. **Delivery**: Is the practice delivering at quality and on time?
4. **Client health**: Are clients satisfied and growing?

---

## Financial Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target / Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) | Total monthly managed service revenue | Growing; predictable |
| Project revenue | Total project invoiced this month | Against target; seasonal variation acceptable |
| Total revenue | MRR + project revenue | Growing YoY |
| Gross margin | (Revenue - Direct costs) ÷ Revenue | >40% blended; >50% on managed service |
| EBITDA margin | Operating profit before depreciation ÷ Revenue | >15% at operating scale |
| Revenue per head | Total revenue ÷ total headcount | £120k–£180k per head target for technical staff |
| Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) | Average days to collect an invoice | &lt;45 days; DSO >60 days is a cash flow warning |
| MRR growth rate | (MRR this month - MRR last month) / MRR last month × 100 | >3–5% per month for growth-stage practice |
| Churn (MRR) | MRR lost from cancelled/reduced contracts | &lt;2% per month |

### Financial Health Red Lines

- Gross margin below 30%: pricing is too low or costs are out of control
- DSO above 60 days: accounts receivable process needs attention; cash flow at risk
- MRR growth rate negative for 2+ consecutive months: managed service base is shrinking; immediate investigation required

---

## Pipeline Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Total weighted pipeline | Σ (Deal value × Stage probability) | >3× monthly revenue target |
| New opportunities added | Opportunities entering Stage 1+ this month | Consistent with growth targets |
| Pipeline win rate | Closed Won ÷ (Won + Lost) | >35% |
| Average deal size | Total won revenue ÷ won deal count | Growing (indicates upsell success) |
| Sales cycle length | Average days from Stage 1 to Closed Won | &lt;90 days for standard projects |
| Managed service renewal rate | Contracts renewed ÷ contracts due | >90% |

---

## Delivery Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Project on-time delivery | % of projects delivered by agreed milestone date | >85% |
| Customer acceptance rate | % of rooms accepted on first walkthrough (no P1/P2 snags) | >90% |
| Average snag resolution time | Mean days from snag identification to closure | P1: same day; P2: &lt;3 days |
| Managed service SLA compliance | % of tickets meeting response SLA | >95% |
| P1 resolution within target | % of P1 incidents resolved within target time | >90% |
| Utilisation rate (engineers) | Billable hours ÷ available hours | 70–80% (below 70%: overstaffed or underbooked; above 85%: burnout risk) |

### Delivery Health Red Lines

- SLA compliance below 90%: service delivery capacity or tooling problem; immediate investigation
- Engineer utilisation above 85% for 3+ months: team is at capacity risk; hire or constrain sales
- Project on-time delivery below 75%: project management or scoping problem; review process

---

## Client Health Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Average client health score | Mean health score across managed service clients | >2.5 (Green/Amber scale) |
| Clients at Red health score | Count of clients with health score &lt;2.0 | 0; any Red requires immediate action plan |
| Average room utilisation (across fleet) | Mean utilisation % across all managed rooms | >40% 90 days post-deployment |
| Net Promoter Score (NPS) | Quarterly survey: "How likely to recommend?" | >50 |
| Net Revenue Retention (NRR) | Revenue from existing clients this period vs last | >110% |

## The Monthly Management Dashboard

Assemble all metrics into a monthly dashboard reviewed at the practice leadership meeting. Structure:

**Page 1: Headline Summary**
- Month revenue vs target
- MRR trend (last 6 months)
- Weighted pipeline vs target
- Open client health issues (Red count)
- Top 3 achievements this month
- Top 3 concerns / actions required

**Page 2: Financial Detail**
- Revenue breakdown (project vs managed service)
- Margin by revenue type
- EBITDA vs target
- DSO

**Page 3: Pipeline**
- Pipeline by stage (bar chart)
- Win rate trend
- Deals closed this month (won/lost + reason lost)
- Top 5 opportunities in pipeline

**Page 4: Delivery**
- Active projects (vs milestones)
- SLA compliance by client tier
- Open P1/P2 incidents
- Engineer utilisation

**Page 5: Client Health**
- Health score distribution (Green/Amber/Red)
- Accounts requiring action
- NRR trend
- QBRs due this quarter

## Using Metrics for Team Accountability

Metrics are only useful if they are connected to individual accountability. Define personal targets for each role:

| Role | KPI | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Account Manager | New MRR won per month | £5,000 |
| Account Manager | NRR for assigned accounts | >110% |
| Project Manager | On-time delivery rate | >85% |
| Service Desk Engineer | SLA compliance rate | >95% |
| Practice Manager | EBITDA margin | >15% |

Review performance against these targets in monthly 1:1s. This connects the dashboard metrics to individual behaviour — the mechanism through which metrics drive results.

<Callout type="tip">
**Metrics should inform decisions, not replace judgement.** A client health score going Amber doesn't automatically trigger a scripted response — it triggers a conversation. Numbers identify where to look; context determines what to do.
</Callout>
