## Incident Management as SLA Protection

Every managed service SLA is tested by incidents. The difference between a practice that retains clients and one that loses them is not the absence of incidents — all systems fail sometimes — it is how those incidents are managed.

Effective incident management is characterised by:
- Fast detection (via automated monitoring, not client calls)
- Clear ownership (one engineer owns each ticket from open to close)
- Proactive client communication (client knows what is happening before they ask)
- Fast resolution (via well-documented SOPs and appropriate escalation)
- Post-incident learning (the same fault does not recur)

## Escalation Paths

### Tier 1: Service Desk (First Response)

**Who**: Junior engineers or operations staff with Pro Management and Teams Admin Centre access.

**Capabilities**:
- Identify the fault from monitoring alerts
- Perform remote restart of device (Teams Admin Centre)
- Verify sign-in status and resource account
- Check for site-wide network issues (correlate multiple device alerts)
- Apply known-good SOPs for common faults

**Escalates to Tier 2 when**:
- SOP does not resolve the issue within 30 minutes
- Multiple devices at same client affected simultaneously (potential major incident)
- Fault requires Intune policy investigation or advanced diagnostics
- Issue appears to be network-related and requires coordination with client IT

### Tier 2: Senior Engineer (Technical Escalation)

**Who**: Experienced MTR engineers with full diagnostic capability (log analysis, Intune, network tools).

**Capabilities**:
- Advanced log collection and analysis (MTR logs, Event Viewer, Teams diagnostic logs)
- Intune configuration investigation and policy remediation
- Network trace and QoS verification
- Coordination with client's IT team for infrastructure changes
- Remote session on MTR device via TAC remote access or TeamViewer (if enabled)

**Escalates to Tier 3 when**:
- Fault cannot be resolved remotely; on-site visit required
- Microsoft product bug suspected; Microsoft support case needed
- Hardware failure confirmed; RMA process required

### Tier 3: Specialist / Vendor (Advanced Escalation)

**Who**: Most senior technical resource; vendor support channels.

**Capabilities**:
- On-site diagnosis and repair
- Microsoft support case management (escalation through TAC)
- Hardware RMA coordination with OEM (Logitech, Poly, Yealink support)
- Emergency hardware swap from spares holding

## Major Incident Management

A major incident is a fault that affects multiple rooms, multiple clients, or a business-critical event (e.g., a board meeting or client presentation).

### Major Incident Declaration Criteria

Declare a major incident when any of the following apply:
- 5 or more rooms at a single client site are simultaneously offline or impaired
- A P1 fault affects a room during a known high-stakes meeting (pre-registered critical events)
- A fault is caused by a change that affects the entire fleet (Microsoft update, Intune policy change)

### Major Incident Process

1. **Declare**: Service desk or monitoring identifies qualifying condition; major incident is declared in ITSM; incident commander assigned (usually senior engineer or practice manager)

2. **Communicate**: Incident commander notifies client IT contact (phone, not email for P1) within 15 minutes of declaration. Script: "We've identified an issue affecting [X] rooms at your site. We're investigating now. I'll update you every 30 minutes until resolved."

3. **Investigate**: Assemble appropriate technical resource. Senior engineer begins investigation. Parallel track: is this a Microsoft platform issue? Check Microsoft 365 Service Health Dashboard immediately.

4. **Update**: Provide client updates at agreed intervals (every 30 minutes for P1 major incidents). Even if there is nothing new to report: "We're still investigating. No resolution yet. Next update in 30 minutes."

5. **Resolve**: Apply fix; verify across all affected rooms; confirm all devices are operational.

6. **Close**: Close ITSM ticket with full incident timeline documented. Notify client of resolution.

7. **Post-incident review**: Within 48 hours (see below).

<Callout type="warning">
**The worst thing you can do in a major incident is go silent.** Clients who stop getting updates assume the worst — that the practice has lost control. Regular updates, even "no change" updates, maintain trust. A client who receives updates every 30 minutes throughout a 3-hour major incident will often rate the service experience positively — because they were communicated with and felt in control.
</Callout>

## Pre-Registered Critical Events

High-stakes events (board meetings, investor presentations, annual all-hands) should be pre-registered in the managed service ITSM.

When a critical event is registered:
- Engineer checks room health 60 minutes before the event
- Any fault detected triggers an immediate P1 response regardless of severity classification
- A standby engineer is identified for on-site response if needed
- Client contact is on standby to provide physical access if required

Offer pre-registered critical event coverage as a differentiator in the Gold/Premium service tier.

## Post-Incident Review

For every P1 incident and every major incident, conduct a post-incident review (PIR) within 48 hours.

### PIR Questions

1. **What happened?** Timeline of detection, escalation, and resolution
2. **Why did it happen?** Root cause (not the symptom — the underlying cause)
3. **How was it detected?** Via monitoring or via client call? (Client-reported incidents indicate a monitoring gap)
4. **Was the SLA met?** If not, what caused the breach?
5. **What could have been done faster?** Where was time lost?
6. **What would prevent recurrence?** Specific, actionable changes

### PIR Actions

Assign an owner and due date to every action arising from a PIR. Common actions:
- Add a new SOP for the fault type
- Adjust monitoring alert thresholds
- Update the client's network documentation if a misconfiguration was discovered
- Raise a Microsoft support case if a product bug was identified
- Update the spares holding if a hardware failure revealed a gap

Share the PIR summary with the client for P1 incidents — not the internal technical detail, but the timeline, root cause, and actions taken to prevent recurrence. This demonstrates accountability and builds long-term trust.
