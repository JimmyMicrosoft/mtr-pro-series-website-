## The Economics of Managed Services

Managed services have a fundamentally different financial structure from project work. Projects are lumpy — revenue arrives in bursts tied to project completion. Managed services are smooth — revenue arrives monthly, predictably, regardless of how busy the team is.

The critical insight about managed services is that the cost to deliver does not scale linearly with the number of rooms managed. A service desk engineer who can manage 50 rooms can manage 100 rooms with the same tooling and similar effort — because monitoring is automated and most rooms require little intervention. This creates the opportunity for margin expansion as the managed service base grows.

---

## Cost Structure

### Fixed Costs

Costs that exist regardless of how many rooms are under management:

| Fixed Cost | Monthly | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Service desk staff (1 junior engineer) | £3,500 | At 50%+ occupancy on MTR; shared with other work |
| Pro Management tooling | £0 | Included in Teams Rooms Pro license; no additional cost |
| ITSM platform | £200 | e.g., Freshdesk, Zendesk, or ServiceNow |
| RMM tooling (optional) | £150 | e.g., Datto RMM, NinjaRMM |
| Practice management overhead | £500 | Account management, reporting, QBRs |
| **Total fixed cost per month** | **£4,350** | |

### Variable Costs

Costs that increase with room count:

| Variable Cost | Per Room Per Month | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Teams Rooms Pro license | £37.50 | £450/year ÷ 12 |
| On-site visit allowance | £8.33 | 1 visit per room per year at £100 average cost |
| Spares holding (amortised) | £5.00 | Replacement devices amortised over expected failure rate |
| **Total variable cost per room per month** | **£50.83** | |

### Total Cost Model

```
Monthly cost at N rooms:
Total cost = Fixed cost + (Variable cost per room × N)
           = £4,350 + (£50.83 × N)

Breakeven: Revenue = Costs
PRPM sell price × N = £4,350 + £50.83 × N
(PRPM - £50.83) × N = £4,350
N = £4,350 / (PRPM - £50.83)
```

**Breakeven at different PRPM prices:**

| PRPM Price | Variable cost | Contribution per room | Breakeven rooms |
|---|---|---|---|
| £75 | £50.83 | £24.17 | 180 rooms |
| £100 | £50.83 | £49.17 | 88 rooms |
| £125 | £50.83 | £74.17 | 59 rooms |
| £150 | £50.83 | £99.17 | 44 rooms |

At £125 PRPM, the service becomes profitable at 59 rooms managed by a single service desk engineer. Adding a second engineer (incremental fixed cost: ~£3,000/month) raises the breakeven by approximately 40 rooms for the tier 2 engineer.

## Margin at Scale

The power of the managed service model is that margin improves as room count grows, because fixed costs are spread over more rooms.

| Rooms Managed | Monthly Revenue (£125) | Monthly Cost | Monthly Profit | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 50 rooms | £6,250 | £6,892 | -£642 | Negative |
| 100 rooms | £12,500 | £9,433 | £3,067 | 24.5% |
| 150 rooms | £18,750 | £11,975 | £6,775 | 36.1% |
| 200 rooms | £25,000 | £14,516 | £10,484 | 41.9% |
| 300 rooms | £37,500 | £19,599 | £17,901 | 47.7% |

**Key insight**: The breakeven in this model is around 90 rooms. Below 90 rooms, the managed service is loss-making at £125 PRPM with this cost structure. Above 90 rooms, every additional room generates increasing margin.

This is why scale matters for managed services. A practice with 50 rooms under management should be investing aggressively in growing to 100+ rooms — the profitability inflection point is significant.

## Key Financial Metrics

### Managed Service KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Recurring Revenue | Total monthly MSA value | Growing; predictable |
| Average PRPM | Total MRR ÷ Total rooms managed | >£100 and growing |
| Gross margin | (Revenue - Variable cost) ÷ Revenue | >50% at scale |
| Net margin (managed service P&L) | Profit after all costs ÷ Revenue | >35% at 200+ rooms |
| Churn rate | Rooms/contracts lost per month | <5% annually |
| Average contract duration | Mean length of managed service contracts | >24 months |
| NPS (Net Promoter Score) | Client satisfaction metric | >50 |

### Revenue Quality

Not all recurring revenue is equal. Assess revenue quality:

- **Contracted vs rolling**: Annual contracts are more valuable than monthly rolling
- **Tier distribution**: A high proportion of Gold/Premium clients is better than a base of Bronze clients (higher revenue per room; stickier relationships)
- **Client concentration**: If one client represents >30% of managed service revenue, that is a concentration risk

---

## Investment Decisions for Growth

Growing the managed service from 50 to 200 rooms requires investment. Key decisions:

**Additional service desk capacity**: When service desk occupancy on MTR exceeds 80%, hire or allocate more capacity before quality degrades.

**Spares inventory**: As room count grows, increase spares holding. A next-business-day hardware swap SLA at 200 rooms requires at least 5–10 spare compute units, depending on geographic distribution of clients.

**Sales and account management**: Growth requires either account expansion (selling more to existing clients) or new client acquisition — both require investment in account manager or business development resource.

**Partner deal registration**: Ensure Microsoft partner deal registration is in place to protect MTR project and license margin.
