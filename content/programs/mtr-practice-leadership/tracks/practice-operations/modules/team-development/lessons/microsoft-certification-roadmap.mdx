## Why Certifications Matter for an MTR Practice

Microsoft certifications serve three functions in an MTR practice:

1. **Technical capability**: Certifications validate and develop genuine technical skills. An engineer who has passed MS-700 understands Teams administration to a documented standard.

2. **Partner programme requirements**: Solutions Partner status (required for deal registration, NFR benefits, and advanced support access) requires a minimum number of certified individuals at specific certification levels.

3. **Client confidence**: Certifications are visible evidence of expertise. Enterprise procurement teams increasingly require evidence of staff certifications in RFP responses.

---

## The Relevant Certification Path

### Foundation Level

**Microsoft 365 Fundamentals (MS-900)**
- Audience: Anyone new to Microsoft 365 — non-technical stakeholders, account managers, junior staff
- Content: M365 services overview, licensing models, security basics
- Relevance: Good starting point for account managers and non-technical practice staff
- Exam fee: ~£85

### Associate Level

**Microsoft Teams Administrator Associate (MS-700)**

- Audience: Commissioning engineers, IT administrators, managed service desk engineers
- Content: Teams deployment and configuration, voice, meetings, policies, device management
- Relevance: Core certification for anyone commissioning or managing Teams environments; directly relevant to MTR resource account provisioning, policy management, and Teams Admin Centre
- Prerequisite: None (MS-900 beneficial but not required)
- Exam fee: ~£165

This is the **primary certification target** for commissioning engineers and service desk staff.

**Microsoft 365 Administrator Expert (MS-102)**

- Audience: Senior engineers, IT managers
- Content: M365 tenant management, identity (Entra ID), security, compliance
- Relevance: Relevant for engineers who manage client tenants as part of a broader managed service; covers Conditional Access, Entra ID hybrid, compliance policies
- Prerequisite: MS-900 or equivalent experience
- Exam fee: ~£165

### Specialty

**Microsoft Teams Rooms Administrator (MS-720)**

- Audience: Senior commissioning engineers, solutions architects
- Content: Specifically focused on Teams Rooms — device enrollment, configuration, Pro Management, troubleshooting
- Relevance: **Highest-value certification for an MTR practice**; directly maps to the core technical scope of the practice
- Prerequisite: MS-700 recommended but not required
- Exam fee: ~£165

This is the **target certification for senior MTR engineers and architects**.

### Additional Relevant Certifications

| Certification | Provider | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Azure Administrator (AZ-104) | Microsoft | Relevant for practices that manage Azure-integrated environments |
| Endpoint Administrator (MD-102) | Microsoft | Intune device management; highly relevant for MTR fleet management |
| Information Protection Administrator (SC-400) | Microsoft | Compliance-focused clients |
| CTS (Certified Technology Specialist) | AVIXA | AV industry standard; relevant for installation and design engineers |
| CTS-I (Installation) | AVIXA | Specifically covers AV installation — relevant for installation engineers |
| CTS-D (Design) | AVIXA | Covers AV system design — relevant for solutions architects |

## Partner Programme Requirements

To achieve **Microsoft Solutions Partner for Modern Work** designation (the relevant partner status for MTR practices), the practice must demonstrate:

1. **Performance**: New customer adds and monthly active users growth targets
2. **Skilling**: Certified individuals at specific levels within the practice
3. **Customer Success**: Deployment and usage growth metrics

The specific skilling requirements change periodically — check partner.microsoft.com for current requirements. Typically, Solutions Partner for Modern Work requires:

- Multiple individuals holding MS-700 or above
- At least one individual holding an Expert-level certification (MS-102 or similar)
- Specific points earned through Microsoft Learn assessments and certifications

**Why Solutions Partner status matters**:
- Deal registration (protects deal margin from other partners)
- NFR (not for resale) licenses — free Teams Rooms licenses for a demo/lab environment
- Internal Use Rights (IUR) — Microsoft 365 licenses for the practice's own internal use
- Preferred partner benefits — marketing materials, listing on Microsoft's partner directory
- Technical escalation access — advanced support channels

## Building a Practice Certification Plan

### Minimum Viable Certification Portfolio

For a practice with 3–5 technical staff:

| Role | Minimum Certification | Target Certification |
|---|---|---|
| Installation Engineer | MS-900 | MS-700 |
| Commissioning Engineer | MS-700 | MS-720 |
| Solutions Architect | MS-700 + MS-720 | MS-720 + MD-102 |
| Service Desk Engineer | MS-700 | MS-700 + MD-102 |
| Practice Manager | MS-900 | MS-700 (for credibility) |

### Certification Investment

Microsoft certification exams cost £85–£165 per exam. Study materials add £50–£150 per exam. Allow 40–80 hours of study time per associate-level exam.

**Budget**: For a team of 5 achieving the minimum portfolio above: approximately £2,000–£3,000 in exam fees + study materials + staff time.

**Return**: Solutions Partner status generates IUR licenses and deal registration protection worth potentially £10,000–£50,000+ per year depending on deal volume.

### Renewal

Microsoft certifications typically require renewal every 12 months via a free online assessment. Build renewal reminders into the ITSM calendar or HR system.

<Callout type="tip">
**Pay for exam fees and study materials; reward passing with a bonus.** Practices that invest in certification and tie a small financial reward to passing have significantly higher certification completion rates than those that rely on individual initiative. Make the investment visible — frame it as "we invest in your development."
</Callout>
