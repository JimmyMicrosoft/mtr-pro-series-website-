## The MTR Practice Team Structure

An MTR practice sits at the intersection of AV technology, IT infrastructure, and cloud services. The team requires a combination of skills that is genuinely rare — few individuals arrive with deep expertise across all three domains. The practice manager's job is to build a team where these skills are collectively present, even if no individual has all of them.

---

## Core Roles

### Installation Engineer (Junior / AV-focused)

**Primary skills**: Physical installation, cable management, display mounting, AV systems
**Secondary skills**: Basic MTR device commissioning, test call procedures

**Responsibilities**:
- Physical installation of displays, mounts, cameras, and cable management
- Basic device commissioning (sign-in, test call)
- Adherence to installation quality standards and checklists

**Career entry point**: AV installation background (AVIXA CTS or equivalent); transitioning IT support engineers with practical skills.

**Development path**: Progress to Commissioning Engineer by developing Teams Admin Centre and Intune skills.

### Commissioning Engineer (Mid-level / AV-IT)

**Primary skills**: MTR device configuration, Teams Admin Centre, basic Intune, resource account linking
**Secondary skills**: Network troubleshooting, audio/video quality assessment

**Responsibilities**:
- Full device commissioning (configuration, account linking, policy assignment)
- Audio and video quality validation
- Acceptance testing and quality checklist sign-off
- Post-deployment support escalation (Tier 1)

**Key certifications**: MS-700 (Teams Administrator Associate); AVIXA CTS.

**Development path**: Progress to Solutions Engineer/Architect by developing presales and design skills.

### Solutions Engineer / Systems Architect (Senior)

**Primary skills**: Solution design, network architecture, enterprise Intune/MDM, presales technical support
**Secondary skills**: PowerShell automation, DSP integration, commercial awareness

**Responsibilities**:
- Site survey and network readiness assessment
- Architecture documentation and design pattern ownership
- Presales technical support (demo setup, proposal technical sections)
- Complex commissioning and escalation support (Tier 2)
- Mentoring junior/mid-level engineers

**Key certifications**: MS-720 (Teams Rooms Administrator) when available; MS-700; AZ-104 (Azure Administrator) beneficial.

**Development path**: Progress to Practice Lead or Solutions Architect (commercial).

### Project Manager

**Primary skills**: Project planning, scheduling, client communication, risk management
**Secondary skills**: Technical understanding of MTR sufficient to identify dependencies and risks

**Responsibilities**:
- Project planning and schedule management
- Client relationship management during delivery
- Risk register ownership
- Stakeholder communication and reporting
- Delivery against SOW

**Key background**: IT project management background (PRINCE2 or PMP); AV industry experience beneficial but not essential.

### Managed Service Desk Engineer (Operations)

**Primary skills**: Pro Management portal, Teams Admin Centre, ITSM tooling, remote diagnostics
**Secondary skills**: Intune compliance reporting, basic PowerShell

**Responsibilities**:
- First-line response to managed service alerts
- Remote investigation and resolution following SOPs
- Client communication for open incidents
- Monthly report generation

**Key background**: IT service desk background with Microsoft Teams knowledge; AV experience a bonus.

### Account Manager / Customer Success Manager

**Primary skills**: Client relationship management, commercial acumen, upselling/renewal
**Secondary skills**: Technical literacy (sufficient to understand client challenges and escalate appropriately)

**Responsibilities**:
- Managed service client relationships
- QBR facilitation
- Expansion opportunity identification
- Renewal management

## Career Pathway

```
Installation Engineer
        ↓ (12–18 months + MS-700)
Commissioning Engineer
        ↓ (18–24 months + MS-720 / advanced technical skills)
Solutions Engineer / Systems Architect
        ↓ (based on aptitude: technical or commercial track)
                    ↓                           ↓
         Practice Technical Lead        Senior Account Manager /
                                        Solutions Architect (Commercial)
```

The career pathway addresses one of the most common retention challenges in AV/IT practices: talented engineers who plateau because there is no defined development path. Without a pathway, the best engineers leave for Microsoft or larger enterprises.

---

## Critical Hire Sequencing

For a practice growing from 0 to scale:

**Stage 1: 0–50 rooms under management**
- 1 Solutions Engineer (senior; can design, commission, and support)
- 1 Installation Engineer (physical works)
- Project management: practice owner or shared role

**Stage 2: 50–150 rooms**
- Add: Managed Service Desk Engineer (operations)
- Add: Commissioning Engineer (to separate install and commissioning workloads)
- Add: Part-time Account Manager or promote from Solutions Engineer

**Stage 3: 150–300 rooms**
- Add: Second Solutions Engineer
- Add: Dedicated Project Manager
- Add: Account Manager (full-time)
- Establish team lead roles

**The most common mistake** is hiring installation capacity before service delivery capacity. As the managed service base grows, the operational overhead grows proportionally — if the service desk is understaffed, client experience deteriorates and churn follows.
