## Measuring What Matters

"The rooms are working" is a technical statement. "The rooms are being used, delivering value, and replacing less efficient meeting behaviours" is an adoption statement. These are different claims, and clients — especially those renewing a managed service agreement — care about the second one.

Adoption measurement provides the evidence that the investment is paying off. It also identifies which rooms, floors, or teams are lagging and need targeted intervention.

---

## Core Adoption Metrics

### Room Utilisation Rate

**Definition**: The percentage of scheduled business hours during which a room was occupied or booked.

**Formula**: `(Total meeting hours in room ÷ Available business hours) × 100`

Available business hours = Days in period × 8 hours (or client's working day length)

**Benchmarks**:
- <20%: Very low — likely an adoption problem or a room that doesn't serve the local team's needs
- 20–40%: Below average — training or change management intervention recommended
- 40–60%: Good — room is being used but has capacity
- 60–80%: High — healthy utilisation; monitor for booking pressure
- >80%: Oversubscribed — may need additional rooms or a booking policy review

**Source**: Pro Management portal → Utilisation tab, or M365 calendar analytics.

### Meeting Join Rate (MTR vs BYOD)

**Definition**: The percentage of meetings in MTR-equipped rooms that were joined using the in-room device (vs a laptop-based Teams client).

**Why it matters**: A user who books a MTR room and then joins from their laptop has not adopted the room — they're using the room for the space, not the technology. This metric directly measures whether the MTR investment is being used as intended.

**Target**: >70% of meetings in MTR rooms should be joined via the room device within 90 days of go-live.

**Source**: Pro Management portal → Meetings tab → Meeting join method breakdown.

### Active Rooms Percentage

**Definition**: Percentage of deployed rooms that have hosted at least one meeting in the last 30 days.

**Why it matters**: Identifies rooms that are never used — whether due to location, accessibility, training gap, or booking policy.

**Target**: >90% of deployed rooms active within 60 days of go-live.

### Remote Participant Satisfaction Proxy

**Definition**: A proxy measure derived from support ticket volume and user survey data related to audio/video quality in calls from MTR rooms.

**Why it matters**: The business case for MTR often includes improved hybrid meeting equity. If remote participants are still reporting audio/video issues, the investment is not delivering this outcome.

**Source**: IT helpdesk ticket data + periodic user survey.

## Data Sources

### Pro Management Portal

The primary source of MTR-specific utilisation and quality data:

| Report | Data Available |
|---|---|
| Device Inventory | All enrolled devices, status, last activity |
| Utilisation | Per-room meeting hours, booking hours, occupancy rate by day/week |
| Meeting Quality | Per-meeting audio/video quality indicators, join method |
| Alerts and Incidents | Device offline events, peripheral failures |
| Historical Trends | Up to 90 days of utilisation history in portal; export for longer-term analysis |

Navigate to: **pro.rooms.microsoft.com → Analytics → Utilisation**

### Microsoft 365 Usage Reports

For tenant-wide Teams meeting data:
- **Teams Usage Report** (admin.microsoft.com → Reports → Usage → Microsoft Teams): Meeting counts, call quality, device type breakdown
- **Productivity Score** (admin.microsoft.com → Reports → Productivity score): Organisation-level communication and meeting collaboration scores

### Exchange Room Analytics

Calendar booking data via Exchange Online:
```powershell
# Get room utilisation for a date range
Get-MailboxStatistics -Identity "LON-F2-Boardroom@contoso.com" | Select DisplayName, TotalItemSize, ItemCount

# For meeting frequency, use Graph API or Calendar Analytics
# (available via Microsoft 365 admin reports)
```

## Adoption Dashboard

Build a simple adoption dashboard for the client sponsor, updated monthly (or quarterly once adoption stabilises).

### Dashboard Sections

**1. Fleet Status**
- Total rooms deployed: N
- Active rooms (meeting in last 30 days): N (%)
- Rooms in alert/offline state: N

**2. Utilisation Overview**
- Average utilisation rate across fleet: X%
- Trend vs previous period: ↑/↓
- Top 5 most-used rooms
- Bottom 5 least-used rooms (for intervention targeting)

**3. Meeting Quality**
- MTR join rate: X%
- Audio/video quality incidents this month: N (trending ↑/↓)

**4. Adoption Progress vs Target**
- Target: 40% average utilisation by Day 90 → Actual: X%
- Target: 70% MTR join rate by Day 90 → Actual: X%

**5. Actions and Recommendations**
- Rooms below 20% utilisation: intervention recommended
- Rooms with recurring quality incidents: technical review recommended
- Rooms booked >80% of the time: expansion opportunity

## Using Data to Drive Intervention

### Low Utilisation Diagnosis

When a room is consistently below 20% utilisation:

1. **Check booking data**: Is it being booked but not used (no-show)? Or not being booked at all?
2. **Check location**: Is it remote, inconveniently located, or in a space with low footfall?
3. **Check team assignment**: Does the nearby team use meeting rooms? Are they working from home primarily?
4. **Interview the floor champion**: What feedback are they hearing?
5. **Check for technical issues**: Has the room had recurring alerts or incidents?

**Interventions by diagnosis**:
- Not booked: Awareness campaign; floor champion re-engagement; visibility in Outlook room finder
- Booked but not used: No-show policy; check booking friction (approval required?)
- Location issue: Consider whether room serves its intended audience
- Technical issues: Resolve before re-engaging users
