## Training as an Adoption Driver

Training is not a compliance checkbox at the end of a project — it is the primary mechanism through which technology investment converts to business value. A room that is technically perfect but confusing to use will not be used. Usage drives ROI.

Effective MTR training is brief, practical, and role-appropriate. Users do not need to understand how Teams Rooms works — they need to know what to press to start a meeting.

---

## Training Audience Tiers

Different audiences need different training:

### Tier 1: General Users (All Staff)

**Who**: Everyone who books and uses meeting rooms
**Goal**: Confidence to use the room independently for standard meetings
**Duration**: 15–20 minutes maximum
**Content**:
- How to start a scheduled meeting (one-touch join)
- How to start an ad-hoc meeting (Meet Now)
- How to share content from a laptop
- How to adjust audio volume
- How to end the meeting
- Who to call if something doesn't work

**Format options**:
- 15-minute floor walkthrough (group of 8–10 people, in the room)
- Short video (2–3 minutes) accessible from the intranet
- Quick reference card posted in the room (or QR code linking to video)

### Tier 2: Room Champions

**Who**: One or two nominated power users per floor or department
**Goal**: Confident first-line support; can resolve common issues and assist colleagues
**Duration**: 45–60 minutes
**Content**: All Tier 1 content plus:
- Camera controls (manual pan/tilt, selecting between cameras if applicable)
- Whiteboard features (if room has whiteboard capability)
- Room controls (lighting scenes, AV routing if control system installed)
- Common troubleshooting: restart the device, cable check, report a fault
- How to raise a support ticket

**Format**: Dedicated session with project team or IT champion, in the room.

### Tier 3: IT Administrators

**Who**: Client IT team members who will manage the fleet ongoing
**Goal**: Ability to manage devices, accounts, and policies independently
**Duration**: 2–3 hours (may span multiple sessions)
**Content**: All Tier 2 content plus:
- Teams Admin Centre: device list, settings, policies
- Pro Management portal: device health, alerts, diagnostics
- Intune: device compliance, configuration profiles
- Resource account management: password reset, license management
- Windows Update management: ring assignments, deferral policies
- Common advanced troubleshooting (log collection, restart via TAC, remote sign-out)

**Format**: Hands-on sessions with the commissioning engineer at project handover.

## Training Material Design

### Quick Reference Card

The most impactful single training asset. Post it in the room (laminated, on the table or wall near the touch console) or provide a QR code linking to a digital version.

Should contain:
1. **Join a scheduled meeting**: "Touch the meeting name on the screen at meeting time"
2. **Start an ad-hoc meeting**: "Tap 'Meet Now' on the home screen"
3. **Share your laptop screen**: "Connect the HDMI/USB-C cable from the table"
4. **Adjust volume**: "Use the +/- buttons on the touch console"
5. **End the meeting**: "Tap the red phone icon, then 'End'"
6. **Something not working**: "Call [IT helpdesk number] or email [address]"

Keep it to one page, large font, with screenshots of the actual UI.

### Training Video Script Structure

A 2–3 minute room overview video should follow this structure:
1. Opening (15s): "This room is equipped with Microsoft Teams Rooms. Here's everything you need to know."
2. Join a scheduled meeting (30s): Screen recording of tapping a meeting on the home screen
3. Start an ad-hoc meeting (20s): Tapping Meet Now, entering participants
4. Share content (30s): Plugging in HDMI, screen appearing on display
5. End meeting (15s): End button, confirm
6. Get help (10s): Who to call, where to find more information

Record in the actual room with the actual device. Generic Microsoft demo videos do not drive adoption as effectively as client-specific content.

## Training Logistics

### Scheduling Training

- Book training sessions into the project schedule as a named deliverable, not an afterthought
- For large deployments, train floor-by-floor or department-by-department as rooms go live — not all at the end
- Schedule Tier 1 sessions within 2 days of a room going live; users who encounter an untrained room and have a bad experience are the hardest to convert

### Champions Programme

Nominating floor/department champions multiplies training capacity without additional contractor cost:

1. Identify 1–2 willing champions per floor at project kickoff
2. Train champions in a Tier 2 session at handover
3. Champions conduct peer Tier 1 training informally
4. Champions receive a support channel (Teams channel or group email) with the IT team
5. Champions are the first point of contact for day-to-day queries

### Remote Training Options

For multi-site or distributed workforce deployments:
- Live virtual training session (Teams meeting) with screen share of the MTR App
- Pre-recorded training video on the company intranet
- Self-guided walkthrough document with screenshots
- QR code in each room linking to a 2-minute room-specific video

## Measuring Training Effectiveness

Track these metrics post-training:

| Metric | How to Measure | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Room utilisation rate | Pro Management portal usage data | Trending upward within 30 days |
| Help desk calls about MTR rooms | Ticket system filter | Declining after initial 2-week peak |
| Training session attendance | Sign-in sheet / calendar attendance | >70% of targeted users trained within 2 weeks of go-live |
| User satisfaction | Short survey (3–5 questions) | >3.5/5 average satisfaction |

Use these metrics in the post-go-live review with the client sponsor. Declining help desk calls and increasing utilisation are the clearest indicators that training is working.
