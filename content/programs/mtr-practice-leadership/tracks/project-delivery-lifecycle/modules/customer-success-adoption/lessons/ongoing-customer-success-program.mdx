## Customer Success as a Commercial Function

Customer success is not a support function. It is a revenue function. Clients who see clear, quantified value from their MTR investment renew their managed service agreements, expand to additional rooms and sites, and refer the practice to other organisations.

Clients who don't see value don't renew. And in a managed service business, churn is the most destructive commercial outcome — far more costly than not winning new business.

The ongoing customer success programme is the systematic process of demonstrating value, managing health, and identifying growth opportunities across the managed service client base.

---

## The Customer Success Lifecycle

After handover, the customer success lifecycle has three phases:

**Phase 1: Onboarding and Activation (0–90 days)**
Goal: Every room live, staff trained, initial utilisation building.
Activities: Weekly check-in with IT lead, training sessions, adoption monitoring, rapid snag resolution.

**Phase 2: Growth and Value Demonstration (90 days – 12 months)**
Goal: Utilisation at target, value clearly demonstrated, client trust established.
Activities: Monthly status reports, quarterly business reviews, expansion conversations.

**Phase 3: Renewal and Expansion (12+ months)**
Goal: Renewal secured, expansion opportunity identified and progressed.
Activities: Annual review and roadmap session, expansion proposal, contract renewal.

## Customer Health Scoring

Track each client's health score monthly to identify at-risk relationships early.

### Health Score Components

| Component | Weight | Green | Amber | Red |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fleet uptime | 30% | >98% | 95–98% | &lt;95% |
| Average room utilisation | 25% | >40% | 25–40% | &lt;25% |
| Open support tickets (unresolved >5 days) | 20% | 0 | 1–2 | 3+ |
| Last client contact (days ago) | 15% | &lt;14 | 14–30 | >30 |
| Contract renewal date proximity | 10% | >6 months | 3–6 months | &lt;3 months |

**Score aggregation**: Each component scored 1–3 (Green=3, Amber=2, Red=1). Weighted average gives overall health score.

- 2.5–3.0: Healthy — standard cadence
- 2.0–2.4: At-risk — increase contact frequency; identify issues
- &lt;2.0: Critical — escalate to account manager; immediate action plan

### Leading Indicators to Watch

Before the health score deteriorates, watch for:
- Stakeholder contact goes quiet (IT lead stops replying to updates)
- Client raises the same support issue repeatedly (unresolved frustration)
- Sponsor leaves the organisation (loss of champion)
- Client mentions competitor products in conversation
- Contract renewal conversation is delayed or avoided

## Quarterly Business Reviews

The QBR is the most important client touchpoint in the managed service relationship. It is not a technical support call — it is a strategic business meeting.

### QBR Objectives

1. Demonstrate value delivered in the quarter
2. Review any service challenges and confirm resolution
3. Present the forward roadmap (platform updates, new features)
4. Identify expansion opportunities
5. Confirm renewal intent

### QBR Agenda (60–75 minutes)

**1. Opening — Business Context (10 min)**
- Ask about any organisational changes, new projects, or strategic priorities
- This informs how to frame value and identify expansion opportunities

**2. Service Performance Review (20 min)**
- Fleet uptime and device health (percentage online, incidents resolved)
- Support ticket volume and resolution times vs SLA
- Any open items from previous QBR

**3. Adoption and Utilisation Review (15 min)**
- Room utilisation data (Pro Management export)
- Meeting join method breakdown
- Rooms with low utilisation — agreed interventions
- Trends vs previous quarter

**4. Platform Roadmap (10 min)**
- Microsoft Teams Rooms upcoming features relevant to this client
- Windows update schedule and what's changing
- Any planned maintenance or firmware updates

**5. Value Summary (5 min)**
- Total meeting hours facilitated through the platform in the quarter
- Estimated productivity value (optional — see below)
- Headline: "Here's what the investment delivered."

**6. Opportunities and Next Steps (10 min)**
- Any expansion opportunities identified
- Proposed actions for next quarter
- Confirm next QBR date

### Quantifying Value

Clients respond to business language more than technical metrics. Where possible, translate utilisation data into business value:

```
Meeting hours facilitated this quarter: 1,200 hours
Estimated time saved vs travel to in-person meetings: 40% × 1,200 = 480 hours
Average employee hourly cost: £45/hour
Productivity value: 480 × £45 = £21,600 per quarter
```

This is an estimate, not an audit — but it frames the investment in business terms rather than uptime percentages.

## Expansion Playbook

Every QBR should include a structured review of expansion opportunities:

### Expansion Triggers

| Trigger | Signal | Opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| High utilisation on existing rooms | Rooms booked >75% of available time | Additional rooms needed; present capacity analysis |
| New office space or refurbishment | Client mentions new office or floor fit-out | New rooms for new spaces |
| Acquisition or merger | New company joining the organisation | Fleet expansion to acquired sites |
| Positive feedback from senior stakeholders | Sponsor actively recommends to peers | Internal referral opportunity |
| Competitor rooms in another location | Client has Zoom Rooms in one site, MTR in another | Consolidation opportunity |
| Growing remote/hybrid workforce | IT team reports more remote workers | Meeting equity argument for more rooms |

### The Expansion Conversation

Frame expansion in terms of the client's strategic goals, not the contractor's revenue target:

"Your utilisation data shows the three rooms on Floor 2 are at 75% capacity on Tuesday–Thursday. That means your teams are booking in advance and probably not getting rooms when they need them. Based on your headcount on that floor, I'd suggest we model two additional rooms. Would that be useful to look at?"

## Retaining At-Risk Clients

When a health score drops to amber or red:

1. **Increase contact frequency**: Move from monthly to weekly check-ins
2. **Identify the root cause**: Technical frustration? Stakeholder change? Value not visible?
3. **Create a specific action plan**: Named actions, owners, and dates — not generic commitments
4. **Escalate internally if needed**: Account manager or practice leader involvement signals the relationship is a priority
5. **Demonstrate responsiveness**: Fast resolution of the next reported issue is more valuable than any words

<Callout type="tip">
**The best time to prevent a client leaving is six months before their contract renewal.** A client who tells you they are not renewing at the 90-day mark has been dissatisfied for months. The health score exists to catch this before it becomes a decision.
</Callout>
