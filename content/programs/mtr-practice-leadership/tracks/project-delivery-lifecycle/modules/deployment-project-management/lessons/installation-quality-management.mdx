## Quality as a Commercial Imperative

A poor quality installation damages client confidence, generates warranty call-backs, and creates reputational risk. On multi-room deployments, quality problems that are not caught early multiply across the fleet — a cabling method that causes an issue in one room causes the same issue in every room installed the same way.

Quality management is the systematic process of verifying, documenting, and assuring that installation standards are met in every room, before presenting those rooms for client acceptance.

---

## Physical Installation Standards

Establish and brief the installation team on these standards before work begins.

### Cable Management

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| All cables in containment | No loose cables visible; all runs in trunking, conduit, or cable ties |
| Cable identification | All cable ends labelled at source and destination |
| Minimum bend radius | HDMI: 25mm; CAT cable: 8× cable diameter |
| Cable lengths | No excessive slack; cables dressed to fit |
| Power and data separation | Minimum 150mm separation for parallel runs |

### Mounting

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Display mount | Level within 1mm/m; VESA fixings to spec; anti-tilt engaged |
| Display height | Bottom edge 0.9–1.1m from floor (adjust for table height) |
| Camera position | On display or above display; centred on room axis |
| Cable concealment | All cables behind display concealed or in surface trunking |
| Console/touch controller | At table; cable concealed; no tension on cable at use position |

### Power

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Outlet type | Dedicated outlet; no extension leads behind display |
| PoE | Confirm switch port provides correct PoE budget before connecting device |
| Surge protection | Per client requirement; never a standard domestic power strip |

<Callout type="tip">
**Photograph the completed installation from the user's perspective** (standing at the door, seated at the table, looking at the display wall) before the client walkthrough. These photos are part of the as-built record and demonstrate standards were met.
</Callout>

## Commissioning Standards

### Device Configuration Verification

For each room, verify:

```
□ Device sign-in successful (resource account displayed on home screen)
□ Room name displayed correctly on device home screen
□ Calendar integration working (test meeting visible if created)
□ Correct MTR App version installed
□ Windows updates applied (or WUfB policy confirmed in Intune)
□ Intune enrolment confirmed (if applicable)
□ Teams Admin Centre — device appears in Rooms and Devices list
□ Pro Management portal — device appears and reporting data present (if Pro licensed)
```

### Audio Commissioning

```
□ Microphone gain appropriate (speech at normal volume, no clipping)
□ AEC functioning (no echo on test call from remote participant)
□ Noise cancellation active
□ Speaker volume adequate at far end of table
□ No mechanical noise (HVAC, equipment fans) picked up in call
□ Verify audio in both directions on test call
```

### Video Commissioning

```
□ Camera framing covers all seated positions
□ IntelliFrame or auto-framing active (if applicable)
□ No backlight or window causing overexposure
□ White balance correct for room lighting
□ Focus sharp on near and far table positions
□ Display resolution correct (verify in device settings)
□ Content sharing functioning (connect laptop; verify HDMI ingest)
```

### Functional Testing

```
□ One-touch join working (test meeting in calendar)
□ Meet Now functioning
□ Screen sharing from laptop via HDMI/USB-C
□ Remote participant can see and hear clearly
□ Local participant can see and hear remote clearly
□ Display shows remote participant full-screen in call
□ Room shows as available/busy in Outlook room finder after test meeting
```

## The Room Quality Checklist

The room quality checklist is completed by the commissioning engineer for every room before that room is presented for acceptance. It is a non-negotiable gate — a room does not go to client acceptance until the checklist is complete and all items pass.

The checklist has three sections:

1. **Physical installation** (cable management, mounting, power)
2. **Configuration** (device settings, account, policies)
3. **Functional** (audio, video, calling, meeting join)

Each item is: ✓ Pass / ✗ Fail / N/A

Any Fail item is logged in the snag register and must be resolved before the room proceeds to acceptance.

---

## Snag Management

A snag is any installation defect or commissioning failure that prevents a room from meeting the agreed quality standard. Snag management is the process of capturing, tracking, and resolving all snags.

### Snag Classification

| Class | Definition | Resolution Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| P1 – Critical | Room completely non-functional | Resolve before acceptance; same day if possible |
| P2 – Major | Core function impaired (e.g., no audio, camera not working) | Resolve before acceptance |
| P3 – Minor | Non-critical cosmetic or configuration issue | Resolve within 5 business days of acceptance |

### Snag Register

```
Snag ID | Room  | Description                    | Class | Identified  | Owner       | Resolution              | Status
--------|-------|-------------------------------|-------|-------------|-------------|-------------------------|--------
S-001   | R-002 | HDMI cable visible below mount | P3    | 2026-04-15  | J. Smith    | Surface trunking to add | Open
S-002   | R-005 | Echo present on test call     | P2    | 2026-04-15  | A. Jones    | DSP AEC tail length adj | Resolved
S-003   | R-008 | Camera tilt 2° off horizontal  | P3    | 2026-04-16  | J. Smith    | Adjust mount            | Open
```

### Snag Resolution Process

1. Log snag in register immediately on identification (during quality checklist or client walkthrough)
2. Assign to named engineer with target resolution date
3. Update status when resolved: photograph the fix; retest
4. P1 and P2 snags require commissioning engineer sign-off on resolution
5. P3 snags may be deferred to a scheduled return visit if client agrees in writing

---

## The Acceptance Gate

A room is ready for client acceptance when:

1. Quality checklist is 100% complete with all items passing
2. No P1 or P2 snags outstanding
3. All P3 snags logged and agreed remediation plan in place
4. Commissioning engineer has signed the quality checklist

Present the completed quality checklist and snag register to the client IT representative during the acceptance walkthrough. Walk through every room on the floor together — the client confirming acceptance in person is more reliable than signature on a document they haven't reviewed.
