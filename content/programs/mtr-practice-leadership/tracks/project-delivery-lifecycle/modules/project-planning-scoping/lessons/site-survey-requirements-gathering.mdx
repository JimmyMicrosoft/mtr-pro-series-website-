## Why the Survey Comes Before the Proposal

The most expensive mistake an MTR practice can make is writing a proposal before completing a thorough site survey. Proposals written from room dimensions and a headcount figure routinely miss:

- Legacy infrastructure that cannot support the planned design (undersized conduit, no cable routes)
- Acoustic problems that require treatment before technology will perform
- IT constraints that change the licensing model (shared devices, no Entra ID, hybrid Exchange)
- Organisational dynamics that affect the change management scope
- Physical access restrictions that affect installation scheduling and cost

A site survey is not a formality — it is the primary risk mitigation activity for the project. Every hour spent in the survey saves multiple hours in scope change negotiations.

---

## Survey Preparation

### Pre-Visit Research

Before arriving on site, gather as much context as possible from the client:

| Information to Gather | Source | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Floor plans or space layouts | Facilities / Estates | Cable routing, room dimensions, number of spaces |
| Existing AV/VC inventory | IT or AV team | Reuse opportunities, integration constraints |
| M365 tenant details | IT admin | License tier, resource account method, hybrid config |
| Network topology overview | Network team | VLAN strategy, PoE availability, internet egress |
| Meeting usage data | IT or business stakeholders | Room sizing, feature priorities |
| Current pain points | End users, IT | What the technology must solve |

Request floor plans as PDFs or DWG files — do not rely on photographs of whiteboards. Even approximate drawings are more reliable than memory.

### Survey Kit

Arrive on site with:
- Laser distance measure (for accurate room dimensions)
- Network cable tester with PoE detection
- HDMI/DP signal tester
- Torch and mirror (for ceiling voids and under-floor routes)
- Tablet or laptop with survey form loaded
- Camera (phone is fine, but take structured photos)

---

## Room Survey Methodology

### Documenting Each Space

Complete a separate survey record for every room in scope. Do not average across rooms or make assumptions about similar-looking spaces — an identical floor plan can have very different acoustic, structural, and network characteristics.

**Physical dimensions**

- Length × width × ceiling height (measure actual, not drawing — discrepancies are common)
- Ceiling material and construction (acoustic tile, plasterboard, concrete)
- Wall materials (glass, plasterboard, block — critical for acoustic treatment planning)
- Window area and orientation (solar gain affects camera white balance and room temperature)
- Door position and swing direction (affects cable routing and furniture placement)

**Existing infrastructure**

- Cable containment routes and capacity (trunking, conduit, floor boxes)
- Power outlet positions and spare capacity
- Existing network outlets — note port IDs if possible
- Existing AV equipment to be retained or removed
- Ceiling void access points

**Display wall assessment**

- Mounting surface material and structural capacity
- Sight lines from all seating positions
- Ambient light levels at display location (measure with light meter or phone app)
- HVAC vents near display wall (vibration and airflow affect microphone performance)

**Table and seating configuration**

- Table shape, dimensions, and material
- Maximum seated capacity
- Distance from end of table to display wall (camera framing determinant)

<Callout type="tip">
**Photograph everything.** Take structured photos: looking from the display wall toward seating, from seating toward display, both side walls, ceiling, any existing infrastructure. Label photos with room name/number before leaving site.
</Callout>

## IT and Network Discovery

The IT discovery is as important as the physical survey. Work through these areas systematically with the client's IT representative.

### Microsoft 365 and Teams Environment

```
Discovery questions:
□ What is the M365 tenant type? (Commercial, GCC, GCC-High, DoD)
□ What Teams Rooms license tier is the client expecting? (Basic, Pro)
□ Are resource accounts created in cloud-only Entra ID or hybrid AD/Entra?
□ Is the tenant in Islands, SFB Only, or Teams Only coexistence mode?
□ Are there any Conditional Access policies that affect device sign-in?
□ Is Teams Phone (PSTN calling) required in any rooms?
□ Are there meeting room mailboxes already provisioned, or will they be created fresh?
```

<Callout type="warning">
If the tenant is in **Islands or SFB Only coexistence mode**, Teams Rooms devices will not be able to join calendar-scheduled meetings reliably. This must be resolved before deployment — not during it. Flag this immediately if discovered.
</Callout>

### Network Infrastructure

```
Discovery questions:
□ Is a dedicated VLAN available for MTR devices?
□ Is QoS DSCP marking supported end-to-end (switch → firewall → WAN)?
□ What is the internet egress capacity? Is there a bandwidth budget per site?
□ Are PoE switches available at each room location? What PoE budget per port?
□ Is DNS resolution working for *.microsoft.com, *.teams.microsoft.com?
□ Are any proxy servers or SSL inspection in place for outbound HTTPS?
□ Are the Microsoft 365 URL/IP ranges allowed through the firewall?
```

See the network readiness assessment lesson (p4-t1-m1-l3) for the full network validation methodology.

### Security and Compliance

```
Discovery questions:
□ Is there an MDM/Intune requirement for device enrolment?
□ Are there CIS or NIST baseline requirements for Windows devices?
□ Is BitLocker encryption required?
□ What is the patching policy — who approves and schedules updates?
□ Are there physical security requirements (Kensington locks, camera privacy covers)?
```

## Stakeholder Interviews

The physical and IT surveys capture what exists. Stakeholder interviews capture what is needed and what is politically important.

### Who to Interview

| Stakeholder | Key Questions |
|---|---|
| IT Director / CIO | Budget, timeline, success metrics, strategic context |
| End users (sample) | Current pain points, meeting patterns, feature wishlist |
| Facilities / Estates | Structural constraints, access restrictions, planned refurbs |
| Network / Security | Policy constraints, change management process, firewall rules |
| Executive sponsor | Business drivers, political sensitivities, sign-off authority |

### Questions That Surface Hidden Requirements

- "What does a bad meeting look like in your organisation today?"
- "Have you tried any meeting room technology before? What happened?"
- "Are there rooms or floors with specific security or access requirements?"
- "Is there a planned office move or refurbishment in the next 12 months?"
- "Who needs to approve changes to the network?"
- "Are any rooms used for sensitive commercial discussions?" (encryption, BYOD policy)

## Survey Documentation Standards

Every survey record must be filed immediately after the site visit — not reconstructed from memory later.

### Required Survey Outputs

1. **Room inventory spreadsheet**: One row per room with all physical and infrastructure fields
2. **Photo library**: Structured, labelled photographs for every room
3. **Network topology sketch**: Annotated floor plan showing network outlet positions and VLAN assignments
4. **IT discovery notes**: Answers to the discovery question bank, with the name of the IT contact who provided each answer
5. **Issues and risks log**: Every identified constraint, gap, or unknown captured with a severity rating

### Severity Classification

| Severity | Definition | Required Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Blocker** | Deployment cannot proceed without resolution | Must be resolved before proposal submission |
| **Major** | Significant cost or scope impact if not addressed | Must be costed into proposal; discussed with client |
| **Minor** | Manageable within normal project contingency | Noted in risk register |
| **Watch** | No current impact but requires monitoring | Logged for project team awareness |

<Callout type="tip">
**Never submit a proposal with unresolved Blockers.** If you discover a Blocker during the survey, arrange a call with the client IT team before writing the proposal. Presenting a Blocker in the proposal itself — especially in front of an executive audience — damages credibility and stalls the sale.
</Callout>

---

## Red Flags That Require a Pre-Proposal Conversation

These survey findings should trigger a direct conversation with the client before any proposal is written:

- M365 tenant in non-Teams Only coexistence mode
- No dedicated VLAN available and network team unwilling to create one
- SSL inspection or proxy that cannot be bypassed for Teams traffic
- Structural ceiling that cannot support camera/display mounting without significant works
- More than 20% of rooms requiring remediation work before technology installation
- Client IT team with no available resource to provision resource accounts
- Planned office move within project delivery window

These are not automatic deal-killers — but they require explicit agreement on scope, cost allocation, and timeline before the proposal clock starts.
