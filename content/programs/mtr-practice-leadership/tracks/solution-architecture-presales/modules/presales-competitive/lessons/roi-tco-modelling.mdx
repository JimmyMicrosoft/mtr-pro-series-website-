## Why Financial Models Matter in MTR Sales

IT managers evaluate technology on features and specs. CFOs and CIOs evaluate it on returns and costs. If the economic buyer in an MTR sale is a CIO or CFO, the conversation must include numbers.

A credible ROI model does two things: it justifies the investment to the person approving the budget, and it differentiates the solution architect from competitors who only show product brochures.

You do not need to be a financial analyst to build a usable ROI model. You need to understand the cost structure of the deployment and the value categories that the investment generates.

---

## Total Cost of Ownership

The TCO model captures all costs across a 3–5 year lifecycle, not just the initial capital expenditure.

### Cost Categories

**Year 0: Capital Expenditure (CapEx)**

| Cost Item | Typical Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hardware (per room) | £2,000–£25,000 | Depends on room type; use design pattern BOM |
| Installation labour (per room) | £500–£2,500 | Includes physical install + commissioning |
| Cabling and infrastructure (per room) | £200–£1,500 | Conduit, containment, patch cabling |
| Project management | £3,000–£15,000 | % of project value; typically 8–12% |
| Documentation | £1,000–£5,000 | As-builts, configuration guides |

**Annual: Operating Expenditure (OpEx)**

| Cost Item | Typical Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Teams Rooms Pro license (per device/year) | £400–£500 | Microsoft published pricing; volume discounts available |
| Managed service support (per room/year) | £500–£1,500 | Varies by SLA tier; contractor-defined |
| Hardware warranty extension (per device/year) | £100–£300 | OEM extended warranty programmes |
| IT staff time (per room/year) | £50–£200 | Estimated management time × IT cost rate |

### 3-Year TCO Example (20-room standard deployment)

```
Capital costs:
  Hardware (20 × £3,500):           £70,000
  Installation (20 × £1,000):       £20,000
  Infrastructure (20 × £500):       £10,000
  Project management:               £10,000
  Total CapEx:                      £110,000

Annual operating costs:
  Teams Rooms Pro licenses (20 × £450): £9,000/year
  Managed service support (20 × £800):  £16,000/year
  Total OpEx per year:               £25,000/year

3-Year TCO:
  CapEx:                            £110,000
  OpEx (3 years):                   £75,000
  Total 3-year TCO:                 £185,000
  Per room per year:                £3,083
```

Present this as a per-room-per-year cost — it makes the number accessible and comparable.

## Return on Investment

### Hard Benefits (Quantifiable)

**Productivity: Meeting time recovered**

The most commonly cited and most credible hard benefit is time recovered from failed meetings.

Research benchmark: average meeting start delay = 8–10 minutes when technology fails. With reliable technology, this falls to 1–2 minutes. Conservative assumption: 5 minutes saved per meeting.

```
Rooms: 20
Meetings per room per day: 5
Working days per year: 220
Minutes saved per meeting: 5

Total minutes saved per year: 20 × 5 × 220 × 5 = 110,000 minutes = 1,833 hours
Average employee hourly cost: £40
Annual productivity value: 1,833 × £40 = £73,320
```

**Travel cost avoidance**

For organisations replacing travel to meeting with video conferencing:

```
Meetings that would previously have required travel: 200/year
Average travel cost per meeting (train, hotel, expenses): £300
Annual travel saving: 200 × £300 = £60,000
```

**IT helpdesk cost reduction**

AV/meeting room issues typically represent 10–15% of helpdesk volume in organisations with legacy systems.

```
Current helpdesk volume: 2,000 tickets/year
AV/room issues: 15% = 300 tickets/year
Cost per ticket (staff time): £25
Annual helpdesk cost reduction: 300 × £25 = £7,500
(After deployment: assume 50% reduction → saving £3,750/year)
```

### Soft Benefits (Directional)

These cannot be precisely quantified but are relevant to the business case narrative:

- **Improved hybrid inclusion**: Remote employees participate more effectively; decision quality improves
- **Client impression**: Professional meeting technology for client-facing meetings
- **Employee experience**: Reduced technology frustration; better meeting experience scores
- **Talent attraction/retention**: Modern meeting technology is a visible signal of workplace investment

Present soft benefits as directional — "we believe this will contribute to X, but we haven't quantified it because it depends on your specific context."

## ROI Calculation

```
Total benefits (Year 1–3):
  Productivity: £73,320 × 3 =        £219,960
  Travel avoidance: £60,000 × 3 =    £180,000
  Helpdesk saving: £3,750 × 3 =      £11,250
  Total benefits:                     £411,210

Total costs (3-year TCO):            £185,000

Net benefit:                          £226,210
ROI: £226,210 / £185,000 = 122%
Payback period: 185,000 / (411,210/3) = ~16 months
```

<Callout type="tip">
**Never present an ROI model as a precise figure without client data.** The correct approach is: "Using industry benchmarks as assumptions, here's what the return looks like. When you give us your actual meeting volumes, average employee cost, and current helpdesk data, we can refine this model to be more specific to your organisation."
</Callout>

## TCO Comparison: MTR vs Zoom Rooms vs Legacy

When competing against Zoom Rooms, build a like-for-like comparison:

| Cost Item | MTR (Logitech) | Zoom Rooms (Poly) | Legacy VC (Cisco) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hardware per room | £3,500 | £3,200 | £6,000 |
| License per room/year | £450 | £250 | £800 |
| MDM cost/year | £0 (uses Intune already) | £80 | £0 (proprietary) |
| IT management time/room/year | £50 | £120 (separate ZDM) | £200 |
| 3-yr TCO per room | £4,850 | £4,550 | £8,800 |

**Note**: If the client does not have Intune, the MDM cost for MTR is not zero. Model honestly.
