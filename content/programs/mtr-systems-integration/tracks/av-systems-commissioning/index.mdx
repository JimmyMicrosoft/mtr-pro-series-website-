## Overview

Teams Rooms technology sits inside an AV system. The device configuration may be correct and the network may be perfectly tuned, but if the audio is unintelligible, the camera is poorly framed, or the display is too dim to read, the meeting experience fails. This track covers the AV systems layer — audio design, video systems, room control integration, and the commissioning workflows that confirm everything works together before a room goes live.

---

## What you will learn

By completing this track, you will be able to:

- Design a conference room audio system — DSP signal chain, microphone placement principles, speaker coverage, and acoustic treatment
- Conduct audio measurement and acceptance testing to validate intelligibility and gain structure
- Select and position cameras appropriate for the room size and use case, and validate framing quality
- Size and specify display systems, manage video signal distribution, and verify video quality
- Integrate room control systems (Crestron, Extron, AMX) with MTR devices and validate lighting and shading systems for video conferencing
- Execute a complete AV commissioning workflow and produce the documentation required for sign-off
