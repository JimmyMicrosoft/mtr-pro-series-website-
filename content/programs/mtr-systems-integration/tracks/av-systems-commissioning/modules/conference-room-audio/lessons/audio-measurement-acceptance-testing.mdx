## Overview

Acceptance testing converts subjective impressions ("sounds good") into objective data that confirms the audio system meets specified performance criteria. This data protects both the integrator (evidence that the system was delivered to spec) and the client (assurance that what they paid for was delivered). A completed audio acceptance test report is the final deliverable before a room is handed over for operational use.

---

## Measurement Tools

**Measurement software:**
- **Room EQ Wizard (REW):** Free; comprehensive; generates RT60, STI, and frequency response measurements. Requires a measurement microphone and audio interface.
- **Smaart (Rational Acoustics):** Professional measurement platform; widely used in professional AV. More capable than REW for ongoing system tuning.
- **iOS/Android apps (AudioTools by Studio Six, dBx DriveRack):** Adequate for basic SPL and RT60 checks in the field.

**Measurement microphone:**
- Use a calibrated omnidirectional measurement microphone (Dayton Audio EMM-6, miniDSP UMIK-1, or similar)
- The microphone should have a calibration file (supplied by manufacturer) loaded into the measurement software for accurate frequency response

**SPL meter:**
- Required for background noise and listening level measurements
- IEC Type 2 or better; A-weighted measurement (dB(A)) for background noise; C-weighted for peak level checks

---

## RT60 Measurement

RT60 measures how long a room takes for sound to decay by 60 dB after the source stops.

**Measurement method (using REW):**
1. Position the measurement microphone at a representative seat position (not at the end of the table, not the exact center — a typical participant position 1–1.5m from the nearest speaker)
2. Configure REW with the USB measurement microphone as input and the room's speaker system as output
3. Use REW's "Measure" → IR Sweep: generates a swept sine signal through the room speakers and records the impulse response
4. REW calculates RT60 (also reported as T20 and T30, which are extrapolated from 20 dB and 30 dB decay — often more reliable than a full 60 dB decay measurement in noisy rooms)
5. Measure at 3–5 seat positions and average the results

**Acceptance criteria:**

| Room Type | Target RT60 | Maximum RT60 |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Huddle room (< 4 person) | 0.3–0.5s | 0.6s |
| Standard conference (6–12 person) | 0.4–0.6s | 0.7s |
| Boardroom (12–20 person) | 0.5–0.7s | 0.8s |
| Large conference / training room | 0.6–0.8s | 0.9s |

*If RT60 exceeds the maximum, document which surfaces require additional treatment and notify the project manager before proceeding to final sign-off.*

## STI Measurement

STI (Speech Transmission Index) measures speech intelligibility from the speaker system at each seating position.

**Measurement method:**
1. Configure the STI measurement source (REW STI module, or a dedicated STI measurement device like the NTi XL2 Analyzer)
2. Play the STI test signal through the room speaker system at the calibrated listening level
3. Record STI at representative seating positions:
   - Near seat (1.5m from speaker)
   - Mid-table
   - Far seat (end of table, maximum distance from speaker)
4. Record a minimum of 3 positions; document all

**Acceptance criteria:**

| Position | Target STI | Minimum Acceptable |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Near seat | > 0.75 | 0.65 |
| Mid-table | > 0.70 | 0.60 |
| Far seat | > 0.65 | 0.55 |

*Note: STI is measured with the room speaker system playing the test signal. It measures the room and speaker system's contribution to intelligibility — it does not measure the microphone system or DSP processing.*

---

## Background Noise Measurement (NC)

Background noise sets the noise floor for microphone capture and affects STI.

**Measurement method:**
1. Measure with all HVAC and mechanical systems running (occupied condition), all room lighting on, no people in the room
2. SPL meter at A-weighting, slow response
3. Measure at the center of the room at seated ear height (1.2m)
4. Record dB(A) and compare to NC target

**NC measurement:**
NC (Noise Criterion) requires octave-band SPL measurements (125 Hz to 8 kHz). Use a measurement app with octave-band analysis, or REW with the measurement microphone.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Target: NC-35 (approximately 42 dB(A) overall)
- Maximum: NC-40 (approximately 47 dB(A)) — above this, speech intelligibility and automixer performance are compromised

---

## Speaker Listening Level Calibration

Confirm the speaker system is calibrated to produce the correct listening level.

**Method:**
1. Generate pink noise through the MTR device's audio output at a reference level (set digital output to −18 dBFS)
2. Measure SPL at a representative seated position with SPL meter in C-weighting, slow response
3. Adjust speaker amplifier output level or DSP output gain until SPL reads 68–72 dB(C) at the listening position

*Why C-weighting for level calibration:* C-weighting is closer to flat frequency response and better represents the broadband level perceived by listeners. A-weighting underestimates low-frequency content.

---

## Test Call Procedure

Objective measurements do not fully substitute for a live call test. Conduct a test call to validate end-to-end performance:

**Test call checklist:**
- [ ] Far-end participant confirms no echo (near-end talker speaks normally while far-end listens)
- [ ] Far-end participant confirms speech is intelligible at normal conversational volume (no need to lean toward mic or raise voice)
- [ ] Far-end participant confirms acceptable background noise level (no audible HVAC or room noise during silences)
- [ ] Near-end participant confirms far-end audio is clear and at comfortable level at all seat positions
- [ ] Walk the table: speak from the end seat — far end confirms adequate level
- [ ] Test room speaker volume at maximum intended level — confirm no distortion

*Document the test call: who participated, from what location (office/remote), pass/fail for each check.*

---

## Audio Acceptance Test Report

Complete the audio acceptance test report before sign-off:

**Report sections:**

1. **Room identification:** Room name, building, floor, date of test, technician name
2. **System description:** Microphone type/model, DSP model, speaker type/model, amplifier model, MTR device model
3. **RT60 results:** Measurement positions, measured values, pass/fail vs. criteria
4. **STI results:** Measurement positions, measured values, pass/fail vs. criteria
5. **Background noise:** NC measurement, pass/fail
6. **Level calibration:** Measured SPL, pass/fail
7. **Test call:** Pass/fail for each check item; notes
8. **Open items:** Any items that failed acceptance criteria with remediation plan and responsible party
9. **Sign-off:** Integrator technician signature; client representative signature

## Summary

- **Measurements:** RT60 (room reverberation), STI (intelligibility at each seat), NC (background noise), SPL (level calibration).
- **Tools:** REW + calibrated measurement mic for RT60/STI; SPL meter for level and noise.
- **Acceptance criteria:** RT60 < 0.7s (standard conference); STI > 0.65 near, > 0.60 mid, > 0.55 far; NC-35 maximum background noise; 68–72 dB(C) listening level.
- **Test call:** Objective measurements plus a live call test; document both.
- **Report:** Required before sign-off; open items need remediation plan.
