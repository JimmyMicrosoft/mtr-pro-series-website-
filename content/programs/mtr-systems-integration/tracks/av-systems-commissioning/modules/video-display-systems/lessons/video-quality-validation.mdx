## Overview

Video quality validation confirms that the camera, displays, and signal path deliver a high-quality visual experience to both local and remote participants. Unlike audio testing, video validation relies primarily on visual inspection — comparing what the camera captures against defined acceptance criteria — supplemented by a test call to validate the end-to-end experience.

---

## Camera Validation

**Framing:**
Verify that the camera captures the intended view — all seated positions visible, no participants cut off at the edges.

Checklist:
- [ ] All seat positions within the camera field of view
- [ ] No participants cut off at frame edges (with people seated at all positions)
- [ ] Camera height appropriate — camera near eye level; no extreme upward or downward angle
- [ ] Camera centered horizontally on the table (not offset to one side)
- [ ] For PTZ cameras: all presets confirmed and labeled on touch panel; auto-tracking verified with people present

**Exposure:**
Camera exposure should produce clear, correctly-exposed images under the room's actual lighting conditions.

- [ ] No overexposure on faces (washed out) — check with room lighting on
- [ ] No underexposure (silhouette effect) — check if window behind participants creates backlight
- [ ] Camera auto-exposure active and adapting to occupant movement

*Backlight issue:* If a window is behind the conference table and the camera faces the window, the camera will expose for the bright background, leaving participants as dark silhouettes. Remediation:
1. Adjust camera auto-exposure to compensate (if supported)
2. Add window treatment (blinds) to reduce backlight
3. Add fill lighting to raise the face level to match the background

**White balance:**
Colors should appear natural under the room's lighting.

- [ ] Skin tones appear neutral (not orange under warm lighting, not blue under cool)
- [ ] White surfaces appear white on camera
- [ ] White balance is consistent as participants move (auto-white balance adapting correctly)

*Tip:* Place a white sheet of paper in the center of the table and verify it appears white in the camera preview on the Teams Rooms touch panel or Teams desktop client during a test call.

**Focus:**
- [ ] Camera in focus at the table distance — no motion blur or soft focus at seated positions
- [ ] For AI-framing cameras: individual cropped frames are in focus (AI framing relies on correct optical focus)

**Pan/tilt/zoom (PTZ specific):**
- [ ] All presets reach programmed positions accurately
- [ ] No mechanical noise audible during PTZ movement
- [ ] Auto-tracking (if enabled) smoothly follows active speakers without erratic movement

## Display Calibration

Commercial displays are often shipped with default settings optimized for showroom brightness (very high) and oversaturated colors. For conference room use, calibrate displays to accurate, stable settings.

**Brightness:**
- Target: 100–160 cd/m² for a room with controlled ambient light; 200–300 cd/m² for rooms with significant ambient light
- Use the display's built-in brightness control; measure with a colorimeter (X-Rite i1Display, Datacolor Spyder) if precise calibration is required
- *Common default setting issue:* Factory-default commercial displays often ship at 300–400 cd/m² — this is too bright for close viewing, causes eye strain, and reduces panel life

**Color temperature:**
- Target: 6500K (D65) for general video viewing
- Some displays default to 9300K or "cool white" — verify and adjust to 6500K

**Contrast:**
- Set contrast so that white content (presentation slides, text documents) is clearly legible but not harsh
- Avoid maximum contrast setting — it compresses highlight detail

**Display mode:**
- Set to a standard mode: "sRGB," "Standard," or "Presentation" mode
- Avoid "Vivid," "Dynamic," or "Sports" modes — these use exaggerated color and are not appropriate for meeting rooms

## Video Signal Path Validation

Before the test call, verify the full signal path from source to display:

- [ ] MTR device output resolution matches display native resolution (1080p → 1080p, 4K → 4K) — verify in Windows Display Settings
- [ ] No overscan or underscan (display edges not cut off; Windows desktop not larger than display area)
- [ ] Dual display mode correctly assigning participant video to Display 1 and content to Display 2 (if applicable)
- [ ] HDMI ingest port passes local source to the Teams meeting correctly — test with a laptop connected to the table input
- [ ] Display powers on and input selects automatically when MTR wakes (verify display control RS-232 or LAN commands working)
- [ ] Display powers off correctly after room vacancy timeout

## Test Call: Video Quality Verification

Conduct a test call to validate end-to-end video performance. Have a remote participant review the video from the room's camera while the technician verifies the far-end video on the room's display.

**Test call video checklist — camera (what far end sees):**
- [ ] All seated participants visible and correctly framed
- [ ] No compression artifacts at normal lighting (macroblocking, pixelation)
- [ ] Camera framing stable — no judder or excessive image stabilization artifacts
- [ ] For IntelliFrame: individual participant tiles shown correctly for all seat positions
- [ ] For content sharing: HDMI ingest content shares cleanly, readable text in shared presentation

**Test call video checklist — display (what local participants see):**
- [ ] Far-end participant video on display is clear, no freeze or pixelation
- [ ] Participant thumbnails in gallery view are identifiable
- [ ] Content sharing from far end is readable (text legibility at all seat positions — connect to viewing distance validation)
- [ ] Display response time adequate — no visible latency in video movement

**Network quality during test call:**
While the test call is in progress, verify network quality metrics from the MTR device:
- Open the Teams call quality indicator (in-call settings)
- Confirm no packet loss, jitter, or latency indicators appearing
- If quality alerts appear, cross-reference with network validation from Track 1

## Common Video Quality Problems and Diagnosis

| Problem | Symptom | Likely Cause | Diagnosis |
|---------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| Backlit participants | Faces appear dark/silhouette | Window behind table, camera facing window | Check camera direction vs. window position |
| Overexposed display | Washed-out, harsh image | Brightness setting too high | Measure brightness with colorimeter |
| Color cast (orange/yellow) | Skin tones appear warm | White balance set to warm, or warm room lighting | Adjust display color temperature to 6500K |
| Pixelation during motion | Blocks appear in video when someone moves | Network packet loss, insufficient bandwidth | Check CQD/TNAT data |
| Camera freeze frame | Video freezes periodically | USB connection issue, camera driver issue, network | Check USB device in Device Manager; restart camera |
| Incorrect framing on PTZ | PTZ returns to wrong preset position | Preset not saved correctly | Re-set and save PTZ preset |
| HDBaseT display — no image | Display shows no signal | HDCP handshake failure, cable termination | Bypass extender with direct HDMI to isolate |

## Summary

- **Camera validation:** Framing (all seats visible), exposure (no backlight issues), white balance (neutral colors), focus (sharp at table distance).
- **Display calibration:** 100–160 cd/m² brightness, 6500K color temperature, standard mode — not factory showroom defaults.
- **Signal path:** Verify native resolution, no overscan, dual display assignment, HDMI ingest, display control.
- **Test call:** Far end reviews camera; local verifies display; check in-call quality indicator for network issues.
