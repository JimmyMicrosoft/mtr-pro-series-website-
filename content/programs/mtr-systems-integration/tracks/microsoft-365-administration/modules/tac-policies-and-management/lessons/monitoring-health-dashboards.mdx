## Overview

TAC health monitoring is the minimum viable monitoring layer for any MTR deployment. It does not require Pro licenses, and it provides the basic device health visibility that answers the most common question in a managed room fleet: "Is this room ready for its next meeting?" Building a daily monitoring workflow around TAC health dashboards — and configuring notifications for critical events — converts reactive support ("the room is broken, can you fix it?") into proactive management ("we noticed the room has a peripheral issue and fixed it before your meeting").

---

## Device Health Status Categories

TAC reports each device's health status using four categories:

**Healthy (green):** All monitored components are functioning correctly. The device is signed in, the Teams app is running, all peripherals are detected and responding, and recent call quality data is within acceptable ranges.

**Non-urgent (yellow):** One or more components have a condition that requires attention but is not currently preventing the room from functioning for meetings. Examples:
- A peripheral (display, microphone, camera) is reporting a sub-optimal status
- The Teams app version is behind the current release
- The device is not enrolled in a configuration profile when one is expected

**Critical (red):** A condition that is preventing or likely to prevent the room from being used for meetings. Examples:
- The camera is not detected
- The microphone array is not responding
- The Teams app has crashed and is not running
- The display is disconnected

**Offline:** The device is not communicating with TAC. This could indicate:
- The device is powered off
- The network connectivity from the device to Microsoft is broken
- The device is in an update/restart cycle
- A hardware failure

<Callout type="tip">
  Offline status alone does not confirm a failure. Devices briefly show
  Offline during scheduled restarts and update cycles. An Offline device
  that returns to Healthy after a maintenance window is not an incident.
  An Offline device that remains Offline after the maintenance window
  requires investigation.
</Callout>

## Peripheral Health Reporting

TAC reports the health of peripherals connected to or integrated with the MTR device:

- **Display(s):** Connected and detected
- **Camera:** Detected and providing video signal
- **Microphone:** Detected and responding
- **Speaker:** Detected and configured
- **HDMI ingest:** Detected (if the device supports HDMI ingest)

**Peripheral health display:** In the device detail view, the **Peripherals** section shows each component with a green/red/yellow indicator. A red camera or microphone indicator while the device itself shows Healthy indicates a specific hardware issue — the Teams app is running but a specific peripheral has failed.

**Android MTR peripheral reporting:** For all-in-one Android bar devices, peripheral health is typically reported at the device level rather than per-component, since camera, microphone, and speaker are all integrated. A healthy Android device status implies all integrated components are functioning.

## Configuring TAC Notifications

TAC can send email notifications when device health events occur:

**Setting up notifications:**
1. In TAC, navigate to **Notifications & alerts → Rules**
2. Click **+ Add**
3. Configure:
   - **Rule name:** e.g., "Critical Device Health Alert"
   - **Condition:** Device health status changes to Critical (or Offline)
   - **Scope:** All devices, or filtered by tag (e.g., `service:managed`)
   - **Action:** Send email notification to specified recipients

**Recommended notification rules:**

| Rule | Condition | Recipients |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Critical Health Alert | Health → Critical | IT support team, on-call |
| Extended Offline Alert | Device offline for > 60 minutes | IT support team |
| Camera Failure | Camera peripheral → Non-healthy | AV support team |
| App Version Alert | App version > 2 releases behind | IT operations |

**Alert fatigue management:** Do not create notifications for Non-urgent status changes to the entire fleet — this generates high volume with low actionability. Reserve email notifications for Critical and extended Offline conditions. Check Non-urgent status during the daily monitoring review.

## Daily Monitoring Workflow

A disciplined daily health check takes 5–10 minutes and catches most issues before they affect scheduled meetings:

**Morning check (before 08:00):**
1. Open TAC → Teams Devices → Rooms on Windows (and Rooms on Android)
2. Apply filter: **Health: Critical** — should be zero. Any critical device requires immediate action before the business day begins
3. Apply filter: **Health: Offline** AND cross-reference against maintenance window schedule — devices that should have completed overnight updates and are still offline require investigation
4. Apply filter: **Health: Non-urgent** — review list; triage against today's meeting schedule; prioritize rooms with meetings scheduled before the end of day

**Weekly review (Monday):**
1. Check app version distribution — filter to devices more than one version behind current release
2. Review update policy compliance — confirm maintenance windows ran successfully over the weekend
3. Check any open Non-urgent items from the previous week — confirm resolution or escalate

**Export and documentation:**
For managed service accounts, export the health status to CSV weekly and include in client reporting. The trend of Healthy/Non-urgent/Critical counts over time demonstrates the effectiveness of proactive management.

## Summary

- **Health categories:** Healthy (green), Non-urgent (yellow), Critical (red), Offline — check Critical and extended Offline daily.
- **Peripheral health:** Camera, microphone, speaker, display — each reported separately in device detail.
- **Notifications:** Configure email alerts for Critical and extended Offline; avoid Non-urgent fleet-wide alerts.
- **Daily workflow:** Morning check (Critical → Offline → Non-urgent vs. today's schedule); weekly review (version compliance, open items).
