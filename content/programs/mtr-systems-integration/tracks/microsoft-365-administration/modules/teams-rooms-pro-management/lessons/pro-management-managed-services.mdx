## Overview

Pro Management was built with managed services delivery in mind. The multi-tenant access model, automated incident detection, remote management capabilities, and analytics reporting are all aligned to what a managed service provider needs to deliver proactive MTR support at scale. This lesson outlines how to structure a managed services operation around Pro Management — from client onboarding through ongoing operational delivery and SLA reporting.

---

## The Managed Services Model with Pro Management

A typical Pro Management-based managed services operation has three service tiers aligned to how deeply the MSP monitors and manages each client:

**Tier 1 — Monitoring Only:**
- Pro Management access provisioned; incident notifications configured
- MSP receives alerts; client IT team resolves issues
- MSP provides monthly health reports
- Value: early warning that the client's IT team acts on

**Tier 2 — Remote Managed:**
- Pro Management access provisioned; incidents assigned to MSP operator queue
- MSP resolves incidents remotely (restart, log collection, sign-in repair)
- Site visits dispatched when remote resolution fails
- SLA: Mean time to resolution (MTTR) for remote incidents
- Value: proactive issue resolution, reduced IT burden on client

**Tier 3 — Full Managed:**
- All Tier 2 capabilities plus proactive maintenance
- Scheduled maintenance windows, firmware updates, configuration compliance
- Monthly client business review with utilization and quality analytics
- SLA: Room readiness rate + MTTR
- Value: complete outsourced room management

## Client Onboarding Workflow

**Step 1 — Delegated access provisioning:**
The client's Teams admin grants the MSP operator account Managed Services access in their Pro Management tenant:
1. Client navigates to `portal.rooms.microsoft.com → Settings → Partners`
2. Adds the MSP's partner account with the appropriate role (Operator or Administrator)
3. MSP operator now sees the client's rooms in their unified multi-tenant view

**Step 2 — Building structure setup:**
Configure the client's building structure in Pro Management → Settings → Buildings. Add each building with correct address and assign rooms to buildings. This enables building-level health views and alert scoping.

**Step 3 — Alert rules configuration:**
Set up the client's alert rules per the agreed service tier:
- Tier 1: Email notifications only to client IT team
- Tier 2/3: Email + webhook to MSP ticketing system + escalation to client

**Step 4 — Baseline establishment:**
Run the first 30 days as a monitoring period to establish baseline utilization and quality metrics. The first monthly report compares against this baseline.

## SLA Metrics Supported by Pro Management

| SLA Metric | Pro Management Data Source |
|------------|--------------------------|
| Room readiness rate | Analytics → Room Readiness % |
| MTTR (remote incidents) | Incident timestamps: detected → resolved |
| Incidents resolved remotely | Incident count by resolution type |
| Poor meeting rate | Analytics → Meeting quality |
| Monthly incident count | Incidents → filter by date range |

**SLA example language:**
- "99% room readiness rate (averaged across the managed fleet)"
- "< 4-hour MTTR for Critical incidents during business hours"
- "70% of Critical incidents resolved remotely without site visit"

These commitments are measurable directly from Pro Management data, enabling objective SLA reporting.

## Demonstrating Value to Clients

Clients who have not experienced proactive monitoring often underestimate its value — they compare the managed service cost to "nothing bad has happened recently." The Pro Management data tells a different story:

- **Incidents prevented from impacting meetings:** Show incidents that were detected and resolved outside of meeting hours (e.g., detected at 03:00, resolved at 06:30, no meeting impact)
- **Remote resolution rate:** "X incidents resolved remotely, saving Y on-site visits at $Z per visit"
- **Utilization insights:** Capacity planning recommendations from utilization data demonstrate value beyond technical support
- **Room readiness trend:** Show readiness rate improving over the managed service period vs. the baseline

The monthly client report (built from Pro Management analytics) is the primary value demonstration vehicle. Each report should quantify what the managed service prevented, not just what it fixed.

## Summary

- **Service tiers:** Monitoring → Remote Managed → Full Managed — match tier to client's IT capability and budget.
- **Onboarding:** Delegated access → building structure → alert rules → 30-day baseline.
- **SLA metrics:** Readiness rate, MTTR, remote resolution rate — all measurable from Pro Management data.
- **Value demonstration:** Incidents prevented from meeting impact + remote resolution savings + utilization insights.
