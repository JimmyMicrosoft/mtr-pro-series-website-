## Overview

A Teams Rooms device is only as reliable as the network it sits on. The most common causes of persistent meeting quality issues — audio cutting out, video freezing, sign-in failures, and missed meetings — trace back to network configuration problems, not hardware failures. This track builds the network knowledge required to design, configure, validate, and diagnose the network layer for enterprise MTR deployments.

---

## What you will learn

By completing this track, you will be able to:

- Design the network topology for an MTR deployment — VLANs, subnets, firewall rules, and QoS policy structure
- Configure DSCP-based QoS marking on switches and endpoint devices to prioritize Teams media traffic
- Validate Wi-Fi networks for MTR suitability and identify configuration issues that cause wireless meeting quality problems
- Model bandwidth requirements for a deployment and verify network capacity against meeting load
- Use the Teams Call Quality Dashboard to identify and investigate quality trends across a fleet
- Diagnose and resolve the most common network-layer problems — proxy issues, firewall blocks, DNS failures, NTP drift, and TLS inspection conflicts
