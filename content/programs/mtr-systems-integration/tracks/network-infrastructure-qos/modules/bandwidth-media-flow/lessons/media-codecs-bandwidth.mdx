## Overview

Teams media quality and network bandwidth consumption are directly linked — but the relationship is adaptive, not fixed. Teams constantly measures network conditions and adjusts codec parameters to balance quality against available bandwidth. Understanding the codecs and their bandwidth ranges allows integrators to plan with accurate numbers rather than worst-case assumptions, and to explain to clients why bandwidth consumption varies between meetings.

---

## Audio Codecs

Teams uses two primary audio codecs:

**SILK (Skype in-line codec):** A wideband codec developed by Skype, used for standard audio in Teams calls. Operates at 8–32 kHz sample rates. Bandwidth: approximately **20–40 kbps** per direction for voice.

**Opus:** An open standard codec with excellent quality at low bitrates. Teams uses Opus for high-quality audio scenarios including music-on-hold and some Teams Rooms configurations. Bandwidth: **6–510 kbps** (voice mode: 6–20 kbps per direction).

**Practical planning figure for Teams audio:** **30–100 kbps symmetrical per room** including RTP overhead, depending on audio quality and number of active speakers. For MTR bandwidth planning, use **100 kbps symmetrical** as a conservative per-room audio budget that provides headroom for all audio scenarios.

Teams audio is extremely resilient — it is designed to maintain intelligibility on poor connections with packet loss up to 30% and latency up to 500ms (though quality degrades significantly at those levels). For planning purposes, audio is the least bandwidth-intensive component.

---

## Video Codecs

Teams uses multiple video codecs, selected based on device capability and negotiation:

**H.264 (AVC):** The baseline video codec, supported by virtually all Teams-capable hardware. MTR devices use H.264 for compatibility with all call types including federation with Zoom and Webex through Direct Guest Join.

**H.265 (HEVC):** More efficient than H.264 at equivalent quality — roughly 40–50% better compression. Used in some Teams-to-Teams calls when both endpoints support it.

**AV1:** The newest and most efficient codec Teams supports. Provides equivalent quality to H.264 at approximately half the bitrate. Requires hardware AV1 support on both endpoints. Available on newer MTR Windows compute units with compatible hardware encoders.

**VP9:** Used in some browser-based Teams scenarios.

**Video bandwidth ranges by resolution:**

| Resolution | Typical Range | Quality Description |
|------------|--------------|---------------------|
| 240p | 200–500 kbps | Minimum quality |
| 360p | 500–1,000 kbps | Acceptable quality |
| 540p | 1,000–1,500 kbps | Good quality |
| 720p (HD) | 1,500–2,500 kbps | High quality — typical Teams Rooms target |
| 1080p (Full HD) | 3,000–4,500 kbps | Excellent quality |
| 1080p outgoing (MTR room camera) | 4,000–7,000 kbps | Teams Rooms main camera stream |

**Per-room video budget for planning:** For an MTR room sending 1080p outgoing video and receiving one HD video stream (typical for a room-to-room meeting), budget **4–8 Mbps** symmetrical. For a multi-party call with multiple incoming video streams (gallery view), budget **10–15 Mbps** to support high-quality reception.

---

## Content Sharing Bandwidth

Screen sharing and content camera streams use separate bandwidth from the main video:

**Screen sharing (application/desktop share):** 500 kbps–1.5 Mbps depending on content movement. Static slides use minimal bandwidth; fast-moving content or video in presentations uses significantly more.

**Content camera (whiteboard camera):** 500 kbps–2 Mbps with AI enhancement enabled. Without enhancement, closer to 500 kbps–1 Mbps.

**Planning figure:** Add **2 Mbps** per room for content sharing headroom on top of the audio and video budget.

---

## Adaptive Bitrate Behavior

Teams does not operate at fixed bitrates. The Teams media layer uses Real-Time Control Protocol (RTCP) feedback to continuously measure network conditions — available bandwidth, packet loss, round-trip time — and adjusts the video encoder's target bitrate accordingly.

**How adaptation works:**
- When the network has available bandwidth: video quality increases toward the maximum resolution the hardware supports
- When the network becomes congested or packets are lost: the encoder reduces bitrate, dropping resolution if needed to maintain frame rate, then reducing frame rate if bandwidth continues to decrease
- Audio is the last to degrade — Teams will sacrifice video resolution completely before compromising audio quality

**Planning implication:** The bandwidth figures above represent the operating range, not fixed values. A room with adequate bandwidth for 1080p video will use up to 8 Mbps when the network has capacity. During a congested period, it may drop to 720p or lower and use 2 Mbps. The network must have capacity for the peak (desired quality) scenario, not just the average.

## Summary

- **Audio:** 30–100 kbps per room; resilient to network variation. Use 100 kbps for planning.
- **Video:** 1,500–8,000 kbps per room depending on resolution (720p–1080p). Use 8 Mbps symmetrical for quality meetings.
- **Content sharing:** Add 2 Mbps overhead per room.
- **Adaptive bitrate:** Teams adjusts quality to match available bandwidth. Audio protected first; video degrades first.
